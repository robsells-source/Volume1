This issue (and the next) was not official, but still did serve at the post office of Tchonking. It was done at the request of a printer who was befriended with a stamp dealer in Bordeaux (source: https://www.franceandcolonies.org/philatelist/238.pdf). The overprint also exists in red colour on the values 1 c, 2 c, 4 c, 5 c, 10 c black, 15 c, 20 c, 25 c black, 25 c blue, 30 c and 40 c.

The cancel "TCHONG KING 12 OCT 02" (all other dates between 12 and 31 October exist) was used to cancel whole sheets, but the postmaster refused to cancel them on letters, see https://www.franceandcolonies.org/philatelist/238.pdf for more information. Genuinely used stamps that were used on letters are very rare.

Forgeries:


Fournier forgeries

The forger Fournier has made forgeries of these stamps. In all the forgeries I have seen, the words "TCHONG" and "KING" did not have a "-" between them.
According to an article in https://www.franceandcolonies.org/philatelist/238.pdf many forged overprints exist. All overprints with a missing letter are forgeries according to the aforementioned article. Forged cancels also exist.


Forgery with a "TCHONGKING 3E/27 FEVR 02 CHINE" cancel.
