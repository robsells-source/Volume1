PUNADRA STATE

QUETTA

In 1883 Court Fee stamps of British India were overprinted "QUETTA" (at least the 1 a and 8 a according to the Forbin Guide).

A 8 a violet Foreign Bill was also overprinted in the same way in 1899.


"QUETTA" on a fiscal stamp of British India from 1869 (only this value exists), it was overprinted in 1899. Specialists distinguish perforation 14 or 15.


Stamped paper (top part) with Queen Victoria (eight Annas twice and one Rupee) with overprint "QUETTA.".

RAJASTHAN


Revenue stamp of India of 1948 with "RAJASTHAN 10 N.P." and black bar on 1 a brown.


Court Fee stamps with King George VI, issued around 1958, provisional issue for Rajasthan, bars over old value and new value (50 np). Also with overprint in Hindi "Rajasthan Pachas Paisa" (Pachas = 50).
