(Diagonal 'BENIN' overprint)

The overprint "BENIN" also exists diagonally, also with accent on the "E". Be careful, many forged 'BENIN' overprints exist. Also non-official reprints exist of the values 1 c, 2 c, 3 c, 15 c (blue overprint), 20 c and 25 c.

Forgeries:


Forged overprints taken from a Fournier Album.


Fournier forgeries, both stamp, overprint and cancel "GRAND POPO BENIN 7 NOV 92" forged. In the Fournier forgeries, the perforation does not match perfectly in the corners.


Probably genuine stamps, but with forged "BENIN" and forged cancel "GRAND POPO BENIN 7 NOV 92". Most likely produced by Fournier as well.

The above cancel can also be found in 'The Fournier Album of Philatelic Forgeries'. See French colonies, forgeries for more details on these Fournier forgeries:


Fournier forged cancels, the Benin forged cancel can be found in the 5th row, last cancel.


These forgeries look at first sight like Fournier forgeries, but they don't have the same distinguishing characteristics. However, the cancel "COTONOU 17 NOV. 92 BENIN" does exist in the Fournier Album, however, this cancel appears to have the year "94". They exist pasted on small pieces of paper. These might actually be forgeries made by the stamp forger Oneglia, who also offered an inverted overprint on the 5 c and overprints on genuine stamps. The forged overprint and cancel were also applied to genuine stamps (see the last 30 c and 1 F forgeries).


Fournier Album with forged cancels. The cancel "COTONOU 17 NOV. 92 BENIN" can be found in the third row, first cancel.
