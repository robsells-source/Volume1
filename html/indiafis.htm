Many states of India issued their own revenue stamps. Here an alphabetical list of the stamps I've seen.

 

The Forbin guide (in French) lists these stamps upto about 1910. The Walter Morley catalogue also lists these stamps (a bit more extensive) up to about 1910.

AJAIGARH


1939(?) Court Fee stamp, 1 anna black with arms of Ajaigarh on the left (tiger and dear holding coat of arms). The value seems to have been printed seperately and can thus change position slightly from stamp to stamp. I've also seen "One Rupee" in this design (also in black).


1940(?) Court Fee issue; 4 a blue. I've also seen 2 a violet. 8 a red, 1 R violet.

AKALKOT (AKALKAT)


"AKALKOT STATE COURT FEE": flag in circle, the following values were issued in 1904 (value always in red): 1 a violet, 2 a blue, 3 a brown, 4 a green, 6 a brown, 8 a lilac, 12 a yellow, 1 R red, 2 R orange, 4 R red, 5 R blue.


1900 Flag and value in red "RECEIPT STAMP AKALKAT STATE" or "AKALKOT": 1 a orange and red. Third image with a larger flag. A 1 R 2 a blue and red should also exist. Also a 1 a blue and red with "AKALKAT".


Court Fee stamps, now printed in one color; 6 a violet. I've also seen 1 a blue, 2 a brown, 4 a brown, 8 a light brown, 8 a green, 12 a blue,


1936 issue; 1 a lilac "RECEIPT STAMP"


Top part of a stamped paper of Akalkot.

ALI PURA


1940: 2 a blue, arms in center "REVENUE" at both sides. I've also seen a 1 a lilac and a 1 Re green in the same design.

Some large sized Court Fee stamps also exist for Ali Pura:


I've seen 4 a blue, 8 a blue, 1 R violet, 5 R orange in this design.

ALIRAJPUR


Sir Pratap Singh II

Some large sized Court Fee stamps also exist.

ALWAR


Issued around 1910: Fiscal stamp with inscription "ALWAR RAJPUTANA" with daggers and umbrella design


With arms with lions in the center. Also a violet "RAJASTHAN STATE" circular overprint. Also with a black 2 lined "RAJASTHAN STATE" overprint

Large Court Fee stamps exist for Alwar with the 3 arms designs as shown above (daggers and umbrella, flags or arms with lions).


In this design I've seen: 1 a, 2 a, 4 a, 8 a, 1 R, 2 R; all in light green color.


Two Court Fee stamps, the left one with "RAJASTHAN STATE" in a violet straight line and the second with a circular violet "RAJASTHAN STATE" overprint on the arms. Note that the word "ALWAR" is larger in the 8 a stamp.

AMB STATE


"AMB STATE NAWAB MOHAMED FARIDKHAN"; 1 a red.

AMBLIARA


"AMBLIARA STATE REVENUE"; 1 a red. Two slightly different types seem to exist.


Court Fee stamp "Rs 0-2-0" = 2 a blue, i.e 0 Rupees, 2 Annas and 0 Pies.


Cash Coupon for Ambliara in the value of 1 Paisa, front and back. Issued during a shortage of coins during World War II. With serial number (here 936) on the front.

ATHGARH

I've seen a revenue stamp of India of 1934 with overprint "ATGARH STATE": 1 a brown.

ATHMALLIK STATE

Some of the Athmallik State were made with a typewriter (1935):


"ATHMALLIK STATE COURT FEE"

1944 issue:


8 As green. I've also seen a 4 annas black, 4 As green and a 5 Rupees red stamp in this design (in two types with text in different position).

AUNDH


1934(?) Receipt stamp


1940's: Country name indicated in English in the lower left part. Court Fee stamp here "8" and currcency indicated in Indian characters. I've also seen 2 a, 4 a, 6 a, 8 a, 1 R, 1 1/2 R, 3 R, 5 R, 20 R (all in red), 12 a brown, 20 R brown (all with Indian currency indication). Also "6 As" red, "12 As" red, "1 1/2 Rs." red, "2 Rs." red, "15 Rs." green, "20 Rs." violet. Also 10 R green with value and currency in Indian characters.

AURUNGABAD

Fiscal stamps of India (Court Fees), overprinted "SECUNDERABAD", exist with further overprint "AURUNGABAD".

Also fiscal stamps of India, with overprint "BERAR", then "SECUNDERABAD" and then "AURUNGABAD".

I have never seen any of these stamps.

AUWA (THIKANA MARWAR, Jodhpur District)


Inscription "Thikana Auwa MARWAR Court Fee": "Eight As." black. I've also seen "Four As." lilac, "Twelve As." red and "Re.One" brown.


Used in 1942, size 75 x 42 mm. For very similar stamps, but with a different border, see Chandawal.
