ATTENTION, most of the locla overprints were just made for stamp collectors (often in whole sets on parts of letters)!


'Normal' "Poczta Polska" overprint

The stamps of Germany, overprinted 'Gen.-Gouv Warschau', were used in certain towns of Poland, a further overprint was applied in the towns of Blonie, Brzeziny, Grodjisk, Kalisz, Leczyca, Lowitz, Makow, Ostroleka, Ostrow, Otwock, Plonsk, Poddebice, Pultusk, Sieradz, Skierniewice and Wloclawek. Examples:

Brzeziny:


"Poczta Polska" and eagle in violet.

Grodjisk (Grodzisk), stamps of Germany, overprinted "Gen.- Gouv. Warschau", and further overprinted "POCZTA POLSKA" and 3 bars


Issued for stamp collectors, all values on a card.

Kalisz, 1) Eagle with overprint 'POCZTA POLSKA', 2) Other eagle with overprint 'POCZTA POLSKA' and 3) overprint 'POCZTA POLSKA KALISZ'

I have seen many stamps with all the same cancel: Kalisz 18.11.18.3-4N * * *, (see the two images above). It looks like the overprint was applied when the stamps were already pasted on the letter.

"Polska poczta Ostrow" overprint
