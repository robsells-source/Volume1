Boyd City Express and Boyd's Dispatch (New York)

1844-1860 Eagle on a globe, inscription "Boyd's City Express", various types. This local post was established by John T.Boyd in 1844, later his son took over in 1859. He sold the business to William and Mary Blackham in 1860. These stamps are difficult to distinguish from each other, furthermore reprints and forgeries exist.....
Literature: "A Primer on Boyd's City Express Post" by John D. Bowman.

1844-45 First issue:


This is the first stamp issued by Boyd (1844-45). Note the framelines. Black background.


Forgery made by Scott of this first issue. This forgery has no framelines.


The forger Taylor also made forgeries of this first stamp. This forgery has no framelines, the Taylor forgery has no dot behind "EXPRESS".

2nd Stamp:


2nd stamp issued by Boyd. Note the framelines. Plain background, map on globe.


Forgery of this stamp, made by Scott

4th and 5th stamp:


4th (1845-1848) and 5th stamp issued by Boyd's: black on green and gold on white issue for special occasions (invitations etc.). Inner ellipse consists of two lines, background netted.


Scott forgery of the 4th stamp.

7th stamp


Same as the 4th stamp, but with thicker inner oval directly around the eagle design, no dot behind "CENTS". Exists on darker and lighter green.

11th, 12th and 13th issue:


11th issue (1855) on the left. Reprint of the 11th issue (taken from the bottom left part of the sheet of 25 stamps) on the right. The light green colored paper has often faded. The background is solid.


The 12th and 13th stamps (1856) were made in red and orange on white paper (only used for a few months) using the same design as the 11th issue. The 13th issue was reprinted.


Reprints of the orange stamp (probably made in the 1880's). Forgeries, made by Scott, also exist.
