CHEEVER & TOWLE

Cheever & Towle 7 State St. City Letter Delivery, Boston

Bundle of letters in circle (a similar design was issued by Hale & Co).


Genuine stamps, the "S" of "BOSTON" does not touch the letter below it. 

  2 c blue (1849)

The only cancel I've seen is a red "PAID" cancel.
There are 6 different kinds of reprint/forgeries of these stamps, often in bogus colors.

Reprints:

Reprints were made in sheetlets of 10 stamps, each with their own distinguishing characteristics. The colours used for the reprints were: dark blue, blue, grey, blue on blue, red, red on yellow, red on blue, blue on red.

Position 1 in the reprint has a weak spot in the circle at the right (at 3 o'clock).


Position 3 in the reprint has a break in the letter to the right of "CTS". Here positions 3, 4 and 5.


Position 4 in the reprint sheet: break in the envelope line to the right of "CTS". Here Position 4 and 5 printed together.


Position 5 in the reprint sheet, break in the circle at 8 o'clock, white flaw near the top.


Position 6 in the reprint sheet, small cut in the outer frame at 11 o'clock.

Position 8 in the reprint sheet, weakness in the bottom of the circle.


Position 9 in the reprint sheet, break in line under "ER" of "DELIVERY", weakness in circle at 6 o'clock. Tiny white dot in the blue area above one of the upper envelopes, right above the "S" of "BOSTON".


Position 10 in the reprint sheet, large break in the right hand side of the circle.


Positions 4, 5, 9 and 10 on the reprint sheet.

Later, additional reprints were made by Scott(?) using the same 10 type printing plates, with the same flaws as mentioned above and some additional flaws. These second reprints exist in blue and pale blue:


Second reprint; position 1, as in first reprint: in the reprint has a weak spot in the circle at the right (at 3 o'clock). Additionally a break in the circle at 9 o'clock.


Second reprint, position 2: circle broken bottom left and damages on top.


Second reprint, position 3. Weaknesses in the outer circle at 1 o'clock, cut at 5 o'clock and another break at 5.30. Also a break in the line under "TO" of the upper "BOSTON".


Second reprint, position 5, not many additional flaws with respect to the first reprint


Second reprint, position 7; 'BOSTON" has been changed to "BOVTON".


Second reprint, position 8: weakness in the bottom of the circle. In addition a break at 2 o'clock in the outer circle.

Forgeries:


Scott forgeries; there are constant ink flaws in both the bottom and top part of the white circle.


Taylor forgeries: The lettering is much larger in these forgeries. The word "DELIVERY" almost touches the left hand side of the central square. Also issued in bogus colors.


Forgeries made by Hussey, the "S" of "BOSTON" touches the letter below it and is placed lower than the rest of the letters in this word. The "2" is different, the "R" of "FOR" is positioned differently with respect to the letter to the left of it.


Forgery with the "H" of "CHEEVER" more like an inverted "N", the "7" quite large, the "2" quite peculiar and first "E" of "DELIVERY" weakly printed.

Some forgeries exist with "DILIVERY" instead of "DELIVERY".


Some kind of 'reprint', I've seen whole sheets of these stamps. Possibly reprints made by Willliam B.Hale?

CITY DISPATCH

City Dispatch


(Genuine, image obtained from a Siegel auction)

This stamp was issued in 1851, in St.Louis, only two stamps have survived (this one and one on a letter).

Another stamp with inscription 'CITY ONE CENT DISPATCH' seems to have been issued in Baltimore (colour: black on lilac), I do not have a picture of this stamp (it is extremely rare).

CITY DESPATCH POST OFFICE

City Despatch Post Office


(Genuine on letter, reduced size, image obtained from a Siegel auction: http://www.siegelauctions.com/1999/817/yf817145.htm )

Two stamps with this inscription were issued in New Orleans; black on green and black on lilac. They are extremely rare.

CITY DISPATCH DELIVERY

City Dispatch Delivery (woman symbolising justice, Philadelphia)

 

Only the 1 c black was issued in 1860, this stamp is not very rare. The genuine stamp has thin lines between the words "ONE CENT", "CITY DISPATCH" and "DELIVERY"

All other colours are reprints or forgeries. I have seen the values: 1 c lilac, 1 c black on green and 1 c red.


Scott forgery, rather deceptive, but the thin lines mentioned above are missing.


Taylor forgeries with a very crude face of the lady.


Forgeries, some even with cancels. Possibly of French origin (it seems to exist with French cancels "CHAMBRE ROUSSEAU PARIS RUE LAMARTIN").

CITY EXPRESS POST

City Express Post


(The only copies of the black and red on yellow stamps, both on letter, reduced sizes)


(a black on lilac stamp on letter)

Issued in 1846. Inscription 'CITY Express Post 2 cts' black with ornamental border or 'CITY EXPRESS POST' with a dove (black on lilac or red on yellow). They were issued in Philadelphia and are extremely rare. The black stamp and the red on yellow stamp are unique. There are only 6 black on lilac stamps known to exist. Some of the pictures above are obtained from a Siegel auction: http://www.siegelauctions.com/1999/817/yf817147.htm

For a stamp with inscription 'CITY EXPRESS POST' in the value 1 c see under Adams City Express Post.

CITY LETTER EXPRESS

City Letter Express

This stamp was issued in 1868. Stamps in a similar design exist for the Carnes City Letter Express.

CITY LETTER EXPRESS

City Letter Express


(1 c red, reduced sizes)


(the unique 2 c on letter)

Issued in 1856 for Newark, extremely rare. Two values exist 1 c red and 2 c red (only one known to exist). The design is similar to the Metropolitan Express (see there). Above pictures obtained from a Siegel auction: http://www.siegelauctions.com/1999/817/yf817148.htm

In the forgeries, the "A" of "MAIL" has a flat top, in the genuine 1 c stamps, this letter has a sharp top.
