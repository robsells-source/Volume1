These stamps were printed by the Evening Post. In the 1 p value there are four tiny "A"s in the design, one in each corner. The 1/2 p has "A A B B" in the corners. At the bottom of the sheet the text "EVENING POST, JERSEY. 17/3/41." can be found on the 1 p stamp and "EVENING POST JERSEY JANUARY 1942" on the 1/2 p value (see image below). I've seen a full sheet of 60 stamps of the 1 p value (10 rows of 6 stamps). These stamps remained valid until 13 February 1946.

An enormous amount of first day covers (1st April 1941) seems to exist. The following text from a local newspaper might explain this (see https://www.jerseybunkertours.com/post/the-road-to-liberation-1st-april-1941)
"JERSEY'S NEW STAMP
BIG RUSH FOR FIRST DAY'S ISSUE.
There was an extremely heavy demand to-day for the first issue of Jersey's penny stamps. The Head Post Office had a full counter staff and there was an endless flow of purchasers throughout the morning, many requests being made for full sheets of sixty. The large table in the center of the office, the telegram desks and all other available space was taken up by persons assidiously sticking the stamps on to envelopes already addressed, these enthusiasts apparently being keen on posting and securing the first day postmark"


Forgeries, the pearls at the left hand side border are too large, according to 'Focus on Forgeries' by V.A. Tyler.

I've seen forged sheets consisting of 25 stamps (5 rows of 5).

Isle of Man

1915 Knockaloe local issue, inscription 'Prisoner of War Camp Knockaloe I.O.M'

  1 p red

This stamp was issued in 1915 in the prisoner of war camp of Knockaloe on the isle of Man. The stamps was intended to serve on letters between the different prisoner camps on the island (25.000 German prisoners were on the island). The stamps was issued and forbidden by the authorities on the same day. All stamps except a few were burnt (only the above letter, a sheet of 21 stamps in the museum of Man and 9 stamps have survived). The above letter was recently offered on Ebay (October 2003).

Another picture of a Knockaloe stamps can be found at the following website of Sandafayre: http://www.sandafayre.com/gallery/stamp_3037.htm.
