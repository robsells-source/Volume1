The stamp dealer Paul Kiderlen from Ulm (Germany) was involved in creating hundreds of fancy covers from exotic countries, all addressed to him. He seemed to have been active from about 1890 to 1914. They are either with handwritten address or with a printed address. There is no text on any of these cards and it is suspected that they were send in bulk from the country of origin to Ulm and that a helpful postal clerk cancelled the cards with an Ulm cancel. Some of them still have the unused reply cards with them.

Similar activities exist from Julius Kinze (Dresden or Halberstadt?) and Alfred Rieder.


A Kiderlen card from Tasmania.


Kiderlen cards from North Borneo (even two with the same date!).

Above a Kiderlen card from Tasmania, similar postal stationary exists for Rhodesia, North Borneo, Iceland, Orange Free State, Seychelles, German East Africa, Gibraltar, Gold Coast, Bahamas, Labuan, Liberia, Grenada, Oil Rivers, Trinidad and Tobago, Ecuador, Sarawak, Antigua, Western Australia, Guatemala. Other British colonies and St.Pierre and Miquelon, Belgian Congo should also exist (I haven't seen them).

In fact, it seems that the great majority of all foreign covers of that period offered on Ebay adressed to Ulm (Germany) have Paul Kiderlen as their destination.
