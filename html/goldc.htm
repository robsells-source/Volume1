Forgeries


Very primitive forgery with printed perforations. It is very likely based on or made with the printing plates that can also be found in an illustration in the catalogue of Placido Ramon de Torres "Album Illustrado para Sellos de Correo" of 1879 (information passed to me thanks to Gerhard Lang, 2016). The Gold Coast stamp appears on page 153 of this catalogue.


Here the actual (defaced) printing plate of this forgery. Left as it appears. Right mirror inverted image as it would have been printed.


These are most likely products made by the forger Oneglia.

The above forgery type has a line from the nose to the forehead. In the genuine stamp, there is no such line. These Oneglia forgeries even can have forged watermarks.

The next forgeries are Spiro forgeries. Spiro forgeries are the most common forgeries of this issue. They can easily be recognised by their strange cancels (as on the following sheet), there is no watermark and the perforation is 13 (instead of 14 or 12 1/2). The 'G' of 'GOLD' of the Spiro forgeries is normal, in the genuine stamps this 'G' has a triangular tail:


Here with a "909" cancel, a pattern with squares cancel and a "FRANCO" cancel, which can be found on forgeries of other countries.

Album Weeds only describes the Spiro forgeries. I have only seen the values 1 p blue, 4 p lilac and 6 p orange of these forgeries.

Other primitive forgeries:


In the 1 p, the words "ONE PENNY" are written too squeezed. Also note the very white pearls on the crown. In the 2 p, "TWO PENCE" is also too short.


Forgery of the 1/2 p pasted on a piece of a letter with forged cancel 'B27' (reduced size). The size of 'HALFPENNY' is much too small.

The forger Sperati made forgeries of the 1/2 p and 1 p values. Sorry, no images available.
