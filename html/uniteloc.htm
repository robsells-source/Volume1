ATTENTION: many of the local stamps of the United States are reprints or forgeries! I have never seen an area with more forgeries, reprints and even bogus issues than the locals and carriers of the United States. If I do not specifically state that an item is genuine in this catalogue, then I'm not sure that it is!

Johnson's Free P.O. Box


(Johnsons' Free P.O. Box Philadelphia)

This is a bogus issue, the text reads "JOHNSON's Free P.O. Box U.S. Pres't. No.7 N.10th St. Philad'a, 1865 Printer, Publisher And Stationer.", with the portrait of Lincoln in the center. There are three types of this bogus stamp: one with narrower "JOHNSON'S" text and no comma behind "publisher". The other two types have a similar "JOHNSON'S" inscription, but with slightly different letter types. This stamp exists in black on green or black on red.

Winan's City Post

Apparently one of the oldest bogus local stamps of the United States. There are 15 different types of this bogus issue (source: Mock United States Local Posts: www.rfrajola.com/Mocks/mocks.pdf), apparently first produced by Hussey in 1862:


With dot behind "POST" and smoke coming out of the bomb. Presumably the original Hussey forgeries. They were issued in vertical strips of four stamps (even in a setting with tete beche stamps), each slightly different (for example the position of "OS" of "POST" or a white flaw on the bomb just below the wing, one type also have a dark bottom right corner, a last type has a flaw at the left hand side of the "W"). Hussey made four values of these stamps: 2 c black, 5 c black on yellow, 10 c black on green and 20 c black on orange. Above shown: 2 c type 3 (white flaw under wing), 5 c "OS" placed too close together, 10 c "OS" placed too close together and dark bottom right corner flaw.


Hussey strip of four stamps of the 20 c. Also two strips of 3 of the 2 c and 10 c stamps. Note the different apostrophes on different stamps on the same sheet.


Internal rectangle is rounded. Most likely made by the forger S.A.Taylor. There is no dot behind "POST" and an extra frame all around the stamp. Taylor printed these stamps in black on various colors, but also for example in blue.

This forgery was made by Casey (see 'Philatelic forgers, their lives and works' by V.E.Tyler, 1976, for a short review of his life). Taylor bogus issues and forgeries

Click here for more bogus issues and forgeries of S.Allan Taylor

S.Allan Taylor was the leader of the 'Boston gang'; a group of forgers located in Boston (see 'Philatelic forgers, their lives and works' by V.E.Tyler, 1976, for a short review of his life). His bogus stamps bear inscriptions such as: Kers City Post, Little Wanderers Aid Society, Bowery Post Office, Warwick City Dispatch Post etc. etc. He also made forgeries of local US stamps.

Other literature: 'Bogus United States Stamps - Produced by S. Allan Taylor' by Judy M. Kersey, published in 1983. This 12 pages. Taylor operated from about 1862 to 1891 and made many forgeries and bogus United States stamps. Many of these exist printed in a variety of papers and in a wide range of colors. I haven't had a chance of reading this book myself.
