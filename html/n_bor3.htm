The values 1 c and 2 c exist with overprint 'WAR TAX' (1941)


Japanese occupation 4 c stamp overprinted with Japanese text in one line; all values were overprinted in 1942 (including the two 'WAR TAX' stamps).


Japanese overprint in three lines on 25 c

The values 1 c to 1 $ also exist with a Japanese overprint in three lines. The value 1 c also exists with two lines of small Japanese characters and two larger characters. The value 1 $ also exist with two small lines and two large characters. These were all issued in 1944-45. A fiscal overprint consisting of three vertically aligned Japanese characters in a box exists for the 1 c 'WAR TAX' stamp.

All these stamps exist with 'BMA' (British Military Administration), these stamps were issued in 1945.

In 1947 these stamps were issued with a royal 'GR' crown overprint (all values) and black or red bars across the words 'THE STATE OF' and 'BRITISH PROTECTORATE'.

Postal Stationary


Philatelitically motivated postcard to Adolph Bauer in Erfurt, Germany. I've seen more of such postcards addressed to the same stamp dealer.


Some other philatelic products addressed to Paul Kiderlen Ulm Germany (even two with the same date). This stamp dealer is known to have been involved in thousands of philatelic covers from exotic places.
