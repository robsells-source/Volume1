MOODY PENNY DISPATCH

Moody (Chicago)


Genuine, I've also seen it with a larger "CHICAGO"

"Moody's Penny Dispatch Chicago" surrounded by ornamental border. This stamp was issued in 1856. Only 20 genuine stamps are known to exist.

First forgery


(Hussey forgery of this stamp)

According to me, this forgery has 12 flower like patterns on top and below, while the genuine stamp has 13 of such patterns.


Very similar to the Hussey forgery, but with "C" and "G" of "CHICAGO" different, very faint dot behind this word.

Another, much similar forgery type exists, with the dot behind "CHICAGO" at a larger distance from it.

Second forgery


A bogus design with inscriptin "MOODY'S City Dispatch CHICAGO.", it also exists in many other bogus colors.


Another bogus design, I've also seen it in black on yellow, black on green, black on red, black on blue.

OVERTON & CO

Overton & Co, Letter Express (New York and Boston)

1844 Flying dove, inscription "OVERTON & Co LETTER EXPRESS."


(Genuine, image obtained from a Siegel auction)


(Probably genuine on letter with 'PAID' cancel)

  (6 c) black on green

Reprints/ forgeries:


Scott forgery, I've also seen the value black on yellow. The "C" and "O" of "CO" are of the same size


Taylor forgery. Again, the "C" and "O" of "CO" are of the same size, no dots under the "O".

Note the broad "R"s in the above forgery. The top of the "T" of "OVERTON" is slanting to the right. I have also seen this forgery in the colours black on red and blue.. Another forgery, very similar to the Taylor forgery exist, with more straight wings.


What are these? Circular designs with 'OVERTON & CO EXPRESS' inscription. I've also seen them in the colors black on blue, black on white and black on pink.


Round design "OVERTON & Co LETTER EXPRESS" with messenger in the center. Not sure what is this.

PENNY EXPRESS COMPANY

Penny Express Company

1866 Bear, horse with rider, inscription "PENNY EXPRESS"


Penny Express Express, bear and man on pony

  5 c black
  5 c blue
  5 c red

Two types of forgeries are known.

First forgery

In this forgery, the small "5"s at each bottom are replaced by stars.

Second forgery


Second forgery, "5"s in the corners are solid. I've seen this forgery in the colors blue, black on blue, red, black and black on grey.

Here, the small "5"s at each bottom are solid (not with white centers as in the genuine stamps).

POMEROYS LETTER EXPRESS

Pomeroys Letter Express, New York (1844)


(Stamp on cover)


A letter with both a Pomeroy and a Hoyt's stamp.


Zoom-in of the above letter.

These stamps were issued in 1844 and exist in the colours: black on yellow, blue, black, red and orange. They are relatively common in unused condition. The owner of the company was George E. Pomeroy.

Reprints exist.
