Fiscal stamps

Many fiscal stamps were issued for Great Britain. I hereby give a list of the different purposes: admirality, bankruptcy court, board of agriculture, chancery court, civil service, common law courts, companies registration, companines winding up, consular service, contact note, copyhold, customs, district audit, foreign bill, hair powder, in chancery, judicature fees, land commission, land registry, life policy, match tax, matrimonial cuase fee, medicine labels, mixture for coffee, naturalization, patent, paymaster general's service, perfume duty, pedlars' certificate, police courts, probate courts, public records, sea policies, transfer duty.

Literature: The Forbin Guide (1915, in French)
Revenue Catalogue, part 1 Great Britain and Colonies by Fred. Geo. C. Lundy (1891).

"ADMIRALTY COURT"

Issued in 1855; in the values 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh, 1 Pound and 5 Pounds, all in violet, on either white or bluish paper (perforated 14 or 16).

In 1875 these stamps were replaced by Judicature Fees stamps.

 

"BANKRUPTCY"


Front and backside of a seal with "BANKRUPTCY" inscription. A control stamp with "VR" at the back and a tiny metal part included on the front (to prevent reuse).

 

Some seal-alike stamps were issued from 1869 onwards, either with overprint "BANKRUPTCY" or inscription "BANKRUPTCY" in pink color (also in blue color in 1894).


Here with blue "BANKRUPTCY" and blue dividing lines (often cut off), 1 p plate O, datestamp 2-2-70; 3 p letter E on top (platenumber), datestamp 11-1-71. And 8 p plate B, datestamp 22-10-70.

Perforated, 1870: 1 p, 3 p, 8 p, 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 7 Sh 6 p, 10 Sh, 1 Pound, 2 Pounds, 3 Pounds, 5 Pounds.


Imperforate 2 Sh 6 p red with black borders (mostly cut off here).


1870 issue, 1 Sh red.

1 Sh blue, here used on 28-2-13.

1873 Queen Victoria


1873 Queen Victoria issue.

In the above design were issued (in 1873): 1 p, 3 p, 8 p (all in lilac and brown); larger size: 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (all in green and red); even larger size: 1 Pound, 2 Pounds, 5 Pounds, 20 Pounds (all in lilac and green).
The colours were changed in 1879: 1 p lilac and black, 6 p lilac and green, 8 p lilac and red, 1 Sh green and black, 1 Sh 6 p green and blue, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and lilac, 1 Pound lilac and black, 2 Pounds lilac and blue, 5 Pounds lilac and green, 20 Pounds lilac and red.
All the 1879 values were reissued in 1895, but with the value printed on the stamp.


King Edward VII design

In 1902 a new design with King Edward VII was issued: 1 p, 3 p, 4 p, 6 p, 8 p (all in lilac and brown color); larger size: 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (in green and brown color). And even larger size: 1 Pound, 2 Pounds, 5 Pounds and 20 Pounds (all in lilac and brown color).


King George V design.

1917 With image of King George V: 3 p, 4 p, 5 p (all in lilac and brown). Larger size: 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 7 Sh 6 p, 10 Sh (all in green and brown). Even larger size: 1 Pound, 2 Pounds, 5 Pounds lilac and brown.

1947 With image of King George VI: 3 p, 6 p (all in lilac and brown). Larger size: 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh 6 p, 10 Sh (all in green and brown).

1959 With image of Queen Elizabeth II: 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh (all in green and violet).

 

"BILL OR NOTE"


Embossed revenue with inscription "BILL OR NOTE", here with datestampe 15-4-82 and letter F (platenumber). It exists in many different designs. Also a Minerva head type and an embossed only type.

These are embossed revenues and not adhesive stamps. Usually with a crown design (also exists with two thistles and no crown for Scotland), also with a harp in the upper left corner (for Ireland). Higher values also exist with the head of Minerva. The earliest use I've seen is 1862 (colorless embossing) and the latest in 1922.


Issue for Scotland

"BOARD OF AGRICULTURE"

Issued in 1889 in the values: 1 Sh green and black, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and violet, 10 Sh green and red. And in a larger design: 1 Pound lilac and black and 5 Pounds lilac and black.
These stamps are rare (more common with "SPECIMEN" overprint).

In 1895 the use of these stamps was discontinued.

1856 Queen Victoria


1856 issue, 20 values were issued ranging from 3 p, 4 p, 6 p, 8 p, 1 Sh, 1 Sh 4 p, 1 Sh 6 p, 1 Sh 8 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 4 p, 2 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh 8 p, 3 Sh, 3 Sh 6 p, 4 Sh, 5 Sh, 7 Sh, 8 Sh, 10 Sh, 14 Sh to 1 Pound (all in violet).


These stamps exist with overprint "IRELAND FEE FUND" (see under Ireland).

These stamps were replaced by Judicature Fees fiscal stamps in 1876.

"IN CHANCERY"


Red seal 3 Pounds with "IN CHANCERY" in green overprint, issued in 1875, with letter "E" (platenumber) in the upper left corner. Here with "SPECIMEN" overprint.

This 3 Pounds stamp was the only stamp issued according to the Forbin Guide, it is very rare (unclear even if it exists in used state). The Revenue Catalogue, part 1 Great Britain and Colonies by Fred. Geo. C. Lundy (1891) also lists a 6 p stamp (but with a question mark).

.

"CIVIL SERVICE"

Some seal alike stamps were issued in 1870 in pink with black frameline and blue overprint "CIVIL SERVICE": 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh, 1 Pound and 5 Pounds. They also exist perforated. They are quite rare.


1872 Two colors green and red: 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (see image above). In 1879 re-issued with 1 Sh green and black, 1 Sh 6 p green and blue (1881), 2 Sh 6 p green and brown (see image above) and 5 Sh green and lilac.
In a larger design: 1 Pound lilac and green, 1 Pound lilac and black (see image above), 5 Pounds lilac and green. Usually these stamps seem to have to have a circular "CANCELLED C.S.C." cancel.


1895: green color with value overprinted: 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh.
In a larger design: 1 Pound lilac and green and 5 Pounds lilac and green.

Similar stamps with Edward VII were issued in 1902: 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (all in green). Larger size 1 Pound lilac and green, 5 Pounds lilac and green.

With the image of King George V, I have seen: 1 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh green.

 

"COLONIAL OFFICE SERVICES"

1899: 7 values with Queen Victoria with value overprinted on the stamp; 6 p lilac and blue. Larger size: 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh green and blue. Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and blue.. These stamps seems to be rare.

 

"COMMON LAW COURTS"

An Inland Revenue stamp seems to exist with overprint "COMMON LAW COURT FEE" in red. I have not seen this stamp.

1866 Queen Victoria, inscription "COMMON LAW COURTS"


1866 issue

Stamps in the above design were issued in 1866 in the values: 1 p, 6 p, 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 5 Sh, 10 Sh and 1 Pound (all in colour lilac, each value in a slightly different design). The 1 Sh and 2 Sh are the most common.

The use of these stamps was stopped in 1876.

"COMPANIES REGISTRATION"

1867 Queen Victoria, inscription "COMPANIES REGISTRATION", different frames

The following values were issued: 4 p, 6 p, 1 Sh, 5 Sh and 1 Pound; all in the color violet. In 1881 they were re-issued in lilac (on bluish or white paper).


With portrait of King Edward VII

In 1902 another set was issued with the portrait of King Edward VII in the values: 4 p lilac and olive, 6 p lilac and olive. Larger size: 1 Sh green and olive, 5 Sh green and olive. Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and olive.

1921 With the image of King George V, I have seen: 4 p lilac. Larger size: 1 Sh green and black, 5 Sh green. Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and green.


With portrait of King George VI

1947: With the image of King George VI, I have seen: 4 p lilac, 6 p lilac. Larger size: 1 Sh green, 2 Sh 6 p green and red, 5 Sh green and red. Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and green.

1959: With the image of Queen Elizabeth II: 2 Sh 6 p green and black, 5 Sh green and black. Larger size: 1 Pound lilac and green.

1891 and 1896 issues

These fiscal stamps were first issued in 1891, they have the face of Queen Victoria. There are several designs with different heights. The following values exist: 1 p lilac and black, 2 p lilac and blue, 4 p lilac and purple, 1 Sh green and black , 1 Sh 6 p green and blue, 2 Sh green and blue, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and lilac, 10 Sh green and red, 1 Pound lilac and black, 2 Pounds lilac and blue and 5 Pounds lilac and green.
In 1896 some surcharges were issued: 2 p on 2 p, 4 p on 4 p (all penny values in lilac and violet), 1 Sh on 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p on 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh on 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p on 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh on 5 Sh, 10 Sh on 10 Sh (all Shilling values in green and violet).


With image of King Edward VII

In 1902 these fiscal stamps were issued with King Edward VII in the values: 1 p, 2 p, 4 p, 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh, 1 Pound, 2 Pounds and 5 Pounds. They have all the colour lilac and violet, except for the Shiling values, who have the colour green and violet.

In 1921 these stamps with King George V were issued (11 values, ranging from 1 p to 1 Pound).

With King George VI I have seen: 1 Sh green and red


With Queen Elizabeth II on piece of paper.

With Queen Elizabeth II I have seen: 1 Sh green and red, 2 Sh 6 p green and red.

No longer used after 1971 if my information is correct.
