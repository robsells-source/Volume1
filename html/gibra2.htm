Postal Stationery


Post card of St.Vincent, overprinted with "GIBRALTAR" (2x)


("GIBRALTAR" on Natal)

A registrered mail envelope of Barbados (2 p grey) was also overprinted with "GIBRALTAR" in 1884.

Later, in 1887, Gibraltar issued its own postal stationery in the values 1/2 p green, 1 p red and 1 1/2 p brown. Also a 2 p registration envelope in red exists (other circular design).


A forged 1/2 p green postal stationary.

With the currency change these were surcharged in 1889 with "5 CENTIMOS" on 1/2 p green, "10 CENTIMOS" on 1 p red and "15 CENTIMOS" on 1 1/2 p brown. Also a "20 CENTIMOS" on 2 p registration envelope in red exists (other circular design).

In 1889 also a new set was issued in the same design, but with the currency changed to "CENTIMOS". I have seen the following values: 5 c green, 10 c red (possibly a 15 c brown exists). Also a 20 c registration envelope in red exists (other circular design). I have seen the 5 c green and 10 c red with overprint 'Morocco Agencies'.


20 c registration.

I have also seen similar postal stationery with the image of King Edward VII in the values 1/2 p green and 1 p red. Also a 2 p registration envelope in red exists (other circular design).

Fiscal Stamps

In 1891 some fiscal stamps were issued. The inscription reads "STAMP DUTY". The following values exist: 30 c, 1.25 P, 1.65 P, 1.85 P, 2.50 P (all in colour lilac), 5 P, 20 P and 30 P (all in lilac and red). In 1898 the currency was changes and the following values were issued: 1 p lilac, 3 p lilac and yellow, 1 Sh green, 2 Sh green and blue, 4 Sh green and black, 8 Sh green and red and 20 Sh violet on red.
