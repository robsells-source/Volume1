1878 Queen Victoria, key Victoria fiscal type, here with "CYPRUS"


Provisional surcharge of 1883: 1 Sh on 3 p

The following values exist: 1 p lilac and black, 2 p lilac and blue, 3 p lilac and green, 6 p lilac and green, 8 p lilac and red, 1 Sh green and black, 2 Sh green and blue, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and lilac and 10 Sh green and red.
Some provisional surcharges were made in 1883: 1 pi on 2 p, 4 1/2 pi on 1 p and 1 Sh on 3 p and in 1898 these stamps were surcharged in brown: 1 Sh on 1 Sh, 2 Sh on 2 Sh 2, Sh 6 p on 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh on 5 Sh and 10 Sh on 10 Sh.

1882 Postage stamp with manuscript "J.A.B.' (J.A.Bulmer, postmaster). Used for 'postal surcharge' accounting purposes for postage due items between local post offices and main post office. They are not listed in the Forbin guide, but they are in the Barefoot catalogue. Later an additional "POSTAGE SURCHARGE" overprint was used in addition to the signature.


Revenue stamp with handstamped overprint "POSTAL SURCHARGE" (but no signature anymore). Used for accounting purposes for postage due items between local post offices and main post office. They are not listed in the Forbin guide, but they are in the Barefoot catalogue.
