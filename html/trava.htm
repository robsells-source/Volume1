A misprint: "nO S S" instead of "On S S"

I've also seen these stamps with overprint "SERVICE" (issued after 1920). Stamps overprinted with a large "C F" are fiscal stamps (Court Fees).

Examples:


1906 Postage stamps overprinted "CF" (Court Fees) in red (except fo the 2 ch in black). At least the following values exist: 1/2 ch, 3/4 ch, 1 ch, 2 ch and 4 ch.


Foreign Bill stamps of India with overprint "TRAVANCORE": the following values exist: 2 a, 3 a, 6 a, 8 a, 12 a, 1 R 8 a and 2 R.


1 a red, "GOVERNMENT OF TRAVANCORE" with "R V R V" in the corners.


"TRAVANCORE GOVERNMENT FOREIGN BILL", 1 R red. In the above 'Foreign Bill' design I have seen the values: 4 a green, 8 a green, 1 R red, 1 1/2 R grey and 3 R red.


"COURT FEE" 2 ch brown. Issued somewhere around 1906. The values 1/4 r red and 1/2 r green are mentioned in the Forbin Guide.


Tranvancore revenue overprinted "KERALA" (1949-1950), image obtained thanks to Bob Scott


Another revenue stamp with inscription "KERALA REVENUE".
