SMALL CAUSE COURT


The following values were issued (all in brown with violet text): 1 a, 2 a, 3 a, 4 a, 5 a, 6 a, 8 a, 10 a, 12 a, 1 R, 1 1/2 R, 2 R, 3 R, 4 R, 5 R, 6 R, 7 R, 8 R, 9 R, 10 R, 15 R, 20 R, 25 R, 30 R, 40 R, 50 R and 60 R. The Forbin guide says that the 70 R and 75 R were never used (exept with "COURT FEES." overprint), the 60 R is extremeley rare (only 13 used stamps known).

HIGH COURT APPELLATE TULLUBANA

The Barefoot Guide mentions a Foreign Bill 4 a, a Court Fee 4 a and a Special Adhesive 8 a with "High Court Appellate Tullubana" overprint. The Foreign Bill and Special adhesive overprinted stamps appear to have been listed in the Walter Morley catalogue of 1910 (page 47) as well. Furthermore Walter Morley lists a Court Fee 4 a with a black "TALBANA" overprint, it is mentioned that other values exist. If my information is correct Talbana is the fees paid for summons.

I've seen three Foreign Bill stamps of 3 Rupees, with a red "Court Fee Appellate Tullubana" overprint and red bars on "FOREIGN BILL":


Block of three 3 Rupees stamps with red "High Court Appellate Tullubana" oveprint. These do not appear to have been used?
