Many types of cancels were used in India, the earliest ones consisting of mute cancels with dots or lines.

Literature:
1. Early Indian Cancellations, by Jal Cooper (1948)
2. Early Indian Cancellations and Postmarks, 1852-1884, W. Renouf (1919).
3. Numbers in Early Indian Cancellations by D.R. Martin.

Letter cancels (1873-1884)


Letter (here "C" for 'Calcutta') with numbers, used from 1873 to 1884.

The letters are (http://www.surinder-singh.com/sikh-stamps-collection-sikhs-indian-philately.php)
A - Allahabad (N.W.P. Circle)
B - Bombay (Bombay Circle)
C - Calcutta (Calcutta Circle)
D - Dinapore (Bihar Circle)
E - Dacca (E.Bengal Circle)
I - Indore (Central India Circle)
J - Ajmer (Rajputana Circle)
K - Karachi (Sind & Persian Gulf Circle)
L - Lahore (Punjab Circle)
M - Madras (Madras Circle)
N - Nagpur (C.P. Circle)
O - Lucknow (Oudh Circle)
R - Rangoon (Burma Circle)
S - Shillong (Assam Circle)


This system was simplified to letters only a few years after the introduction, here 'B', 'N' and 'C' for Bombay, Nagpur and Calcutta.
