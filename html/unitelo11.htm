BROAD-WAY POST OFFICE

Broad - way Post Office

Two stamps were issued in black on gold (in 1849?) and in black (1851?). The two images above were found on http://alphabetilately.com/US-trains-00.html (a splendid website with plenty of information on US locals with trains on it). Many forgeries exist, examples:



Hussey forgeries? Here printed together with forgeries of teh American Letter Mail Co on one sheet.

I think the stamp above is printed by Thomas Woods for George Hussey in 1866. Note that the word "POST OFFICE" is written too low and that the "B" of "BROADWAY" is much 'flatter' than in the genuine stamp. The "O" in "BROADWAY" should be elliptic (and not round).

BRONSON & FORBES

Bronson & Forbes City Express Post

One of the images found on http://alphabetilately.com/US-trains-00.html. This local post operated in Chicago in 1856-57, two values were issued: black on green and black on lilac. Be careful, many forgeries exist (the above pictures are genuine, however, the red handstamp is faked). This post didn't use cancels normally, but a black circular datestamp is known.


Genuine, with large 'BRONSON & FORBES EXPRESS POST' cancel.

Forgeries:


I've been told that these are forgeries made by Taylor. Note that the apostrophe behind "FORBES" is missing.
