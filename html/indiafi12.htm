MAHIKANTHA


"Current only in Mahi Kantha" and Indian text: 12 a. Cancelled with a square hole.

I've seen a Court Fee stamp of British India with the image of Queen Victoria with overprint "Current only in Mahi Kantha" and native text (all text vertically); 12 a.

I've seen a Court Fee stamp of British India with the image of King Edward VII with overprint "Current only in Mahi Kantha" and native text (all text vertically); 1 a.

I've seen a Court Fee stamp of British India with the image of King George V with overprint "CURRENT ONLY IN MAHIKANTHA" and native text (all text horizontally); 1 a, 2 a, 4 a.

see also: http://www.indiastaterevenues.com/Templates/Mahikantha.htm

MORVEE STATE (MORVI)


Morvee State, 1899 issue. In this design should exist (according to the Forbin Guide): value in black: 1 a, 2 a, 4 a, 8 a (all blue), 1 R, 2 R, 3 R, 4 R, 5 R (all green), 10 R, 25 R, 50 R (all in red) and 60 R black. The 100 R shown above is not listed there.


"MORVEE STATE"; In this design I have seen 1 a blue and black, 2 a green and black, 4 a violet and black, 8 a dark green and black, 1 R red and black, 2 R blue and black.


"MORVEE STATE" With quite large areas with text to the left and right of the ruler: 1 R lilac.


"MORVEE STATE" Small areas with inscriptions left and right of the ruler. In this design I have seen 1 a red, 2 a blue.


"MORVI STATE"; 1 a blue. I've also seen 2 a green.

MOURBHUNJ


"MOURBHUNJ STATE" on a 1869 Receipt stamp of British India. Court Fee stamps with this overprint are listed under Orissa as Mourbhanj in the Forbin Guide. It is listed under Mohurbhunj in Walter Morley. Is this the same state as Mayurbhajn?
