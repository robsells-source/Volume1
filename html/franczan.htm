The overprint "3 ANNAS ZANZIBAR" was forged by the forger Fournier. Here a page from a Fournier Album with two of these forgeries. The "Zanzibar" overprints were applied on forgeries of British Zanzibar.


A Bristol reprint with printed perforation. They are printed on Bristol paper if I'm well informed and were printed for l'Exposition Philatelique in Paris in 1900. They are very rare.

Postage Due stamps overprinted "Timbre" , "Affrancht" or bars on "CHIFFRE TAXE" are actually postage stamps issued in 1904 (see there).

The very neat cancels on many of these postage due stamp appear highly suspicious to me. I have seen letters demanding the post office of Zanzibar for (large amounts of) cancelled postage due stamps. I presume the great manjority of cancelled stamps is of such origins.
