HESSEN-KASSEL

Lion in a circle, 1853. These stamps became obsolete in 1866 when Hessen-Kassel joined Prussia. They were issued in blue or red.


It looks to me that the outer part and the inner part with the lion were printed seperately. The stamps have a number (for example "Nr 4") and a value (for example "5 SGR").

Many different values were issued. Blue stamps were for general revenue and red stamps for land registry fees (Kadaster). Specialists distinguish different types and plate printing errors.
