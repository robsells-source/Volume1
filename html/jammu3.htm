I find this stamp suspicious since it bears a bars with "G" cancel that was often used by the stamp forger Erasmo Oneglia. He does list forgeries of these stamps in his 1906 catalogue (15 French Francs for 14 pieces). He did though indicate that he did not make these forgeries himself. He might have obtained them from Treherne, since this forgery looks like type 15 in the above shown Treherne Brighton forgery sheet.

Bisected pairs 1 a green with red ink cancel (left part) and 4 a blue with blue ink cancel.

These stamps were printed in sheets of 60 (12 rows of 5 stamps).
The 10 R and 25 R are very rare. Bisected stamps exist which were used locally. Forgeries on brownish paper exist.
See also: https://stampssa.africa/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/Jammu-and-Kashmir-Telegraphs.pdf
Genuine cancels I've seen consist of ink blobs (violet, red or black) and three ring circular datestamps.

The same central design was also used for postcards (1883).


Strange items; 1 a blue, 1 a yellow, 1 a red, 1 a brown and 1 a green..... The cancels do not resemble any genuine cancel I've seen.
