The following stamps are government consulate stamps issued from 1854-85. The hole was put in the stamps before they were supplied to the government consul to stop them being used illegally. There are both large and small holes punched. They are all postally used by the counsul office.

Miscellaneous

Lake Lefroy, Cycle Mail

Lake Lefroy, Goldfield, Cycle Mail; this stamp was printed to be used in mining settlements. It was issued by Hillier and Maskell and ran between Lake Lefroy, Coolgardie and Kalgoorie (from February to June 1897).


(Lake Lefroy Goldfiled, Cycle Mail, Western Australia)

This stamp seems to be described in the book 'Cinderella stamps of Australasia, first edition'. However I have not seen this book personally. Only 43 stamps have survived of the 1000 printed. Forgeries of this stamp exist.

An imperforate reprint was made in 1984, it has "FACSIMILE 1984" printed in two lines at the back.

Fiscal stamps

Fiscal stamps, swan, inscription "DUTY STAMP":


(Reduced sized images)

I've seen the following values: 1 p red, 1 p red and black, 2 p blue and black, 3 p violet and black, 6 p yellow and black, 1 Sh green and black, 2 Sh red and black, 1 Pound 10 Sh brown and black (30 Sh), 5 Pounds lilac and black. Many more values exist, they were issued from 1904 to 1930.

Other revenue stamp, inscription 'INTERNAL REVENUE', swan:

The above fiscal stamp was issued in 1881. The values 1 p, 2 p, 3 p, 6 p, 1 Sh , 2 Sh 6 p, 3 Sh, 5 Sh, 7 Sh 6 p, 10 Sh and 15 Sh exist (all in the same colour: lilac, in the Shilling values the swan is surrounded by a circle) were issued. Als 1 Pound, 1 Pound 10 Sh in lying format.
I've seen reprints of these stamps in blocks of four containing one stamp of the values 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 3 Sh and 5 Sh. These blocks are imperforate or perforated around the outer border of the block of four only. They were apparently made in 1979 since "REPRINT 1979" is printed at the back. I've also seen them in a block of four values with perforation all around and "WAPEX '79" pasted on a minisheet.


10 Pounds, reduced sized image
