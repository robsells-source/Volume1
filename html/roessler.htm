Forgery of the 2.50 M Germany stamp. Also with "Read a copy of STAMP LIFE LATELY?" overprint. Apparently made by A.C.Roessler.


Two oversized A.C.Roessler fascimiles of the 2 Sh values of Toga.

 

The above oversized stamp of the arms issue of Baden doesn't have any value inscription. The inscriptions are different from the genuine stamps. I have also seen this label in blue and orange. The size of this label is larger than the genuine stamps (about 1 1/2 times). I've been told that it was made by A.C. Roessler, who overprinted these labels with advertising messages. He also sold unoverprinted labels to other dealers. I've seen such abels in blue, green and orange, also with a black overprint "WE SELL THE AMERICAN HINGE 15 c per 1000", also a blue label with "WE TAKE SUBSCRIPTIONS to ROESSLER'S STAMP NEWS" in black.

Some forgeries of the 5 Drachma Crete 1905 issue with overprint "USE THE PERMANENT STOCK BOOK WE SELL IT" or "AMERICAN STAMP DEALERS' ASSOCIATION, INC." are also believed to have been made by or prepared for Roessler.

He also seems to have been very active in 'fabricating' first-day airmail letters. When he ran out of genuine covers, he seems to have produced false ones to meet the demand.
