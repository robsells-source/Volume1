French Soudan was a French colony in North West Africa. It issued stamps from 1894 onwards. It was then icluded in other colonies, but it resumed issuing its own stamps in 1921.


A stamp of the French Colonies, general issues, with a "KAYES SOUDAN FRANCAIS" cancel

These stamps were prepared on 12 April 1894 and were exhausted on 18 July 1894. They were used in Kayes and were only supposed to be put on military letters already cancelled. A few mint stamps are known to exist. See: http://www.timbresmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/1erVolet.pdf The forger Fournier is known to have made forgeries of these stamps.


A page with forged Fournier overprints.

The forger Oneglia offered 3 kinds of forgeries of these stamps in his 1906 stamp catalogue. One with a "0,15" overprint on a 1877 stamp. Then a "0,15" on 75 c (as in the genuine stamp). He also offered a "0,15" on 1 F (not "0,25"!).


A cancel used during a stamp shortage in 1894. Inscription "TAXE PERCUE: Manque de Timbres dans la Colonie."
