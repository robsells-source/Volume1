BERARS (BERAR)

British Administration:


1875 Revenue stamps of India, overprinted "BERARS" in red, blue or black.


1882: Court Fee stamp with Queen Victoria with "BERARS" overprint. This is the 8 a value, the Forbin guide also lists 1, 3, 4, 6, 12 a, 1 R, 1 R 2 a, 1 R 8 a, 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6 Rupees.

The Forbin Guide lists more stamps of Brisith India with overprint "BERAR" or "BERARS", some of them extremely rare (or even known to have been issued, but no stamp has survived).

Local Administration:


Example of Berar 1 a blue, Queen Victoria, issued in 1887, two different types (note the placement of the first "A" of "ANNA" and the "T" of "GOVERNMENT"). According to the Forbin guide there are 30 types of this stamp. I've seen color shades from grey to dark blue. Overprints exist with "SECUNDRABAD" or even further surcharged with "Hyd. R.B.".


Berar: 1887 issue; the following values exist: 1 a red, 2 a brown, 3 a black, 4 a violet, 5 a blue, 6 a orange, 8 a green, 10 a brown, 12 a red, 1 R blue, 1 1/2 R brown, 3 R yellow and 6 R violet. 15 different types exist in one sheet (see for example the placement of the first "N" of "ANNAS" and the "N" of "GOVERNMENT" in the above 4 a stamps).

Stamped paper with "HUNDI" exists with the image of Queen Victoria, I've seen 10 a orange: http://www.indiastaterevenues.com/Templates/Berar.htm

BHAVNAGAR


1887 issue (value in numbers) and 1889 issue (value written in words).

The first issued for Bhavnagar were issued in 1887 with the portrait of the ruler with the value in numbers; 1 a red, 2 a red, 4 a red, 8 a red, 1 R blue, 8 R blue, 10 R green, 12 R green, 75 R green, 250 R brown and 1000 R yellow. In 1889 a slightly modified design was issued with the value written in words: 1 a, 2 a, 4 a, 8 a (all in red) and 2 R blue.


1904 issue, "BHAVNAGAR DURBAR" values from 1 a to 1000 R were issued (27 values); I've seen 1 a, 4 a, 8 a, 1 R (all in brown). Other colors were issued: 2 a blue for example


Later issue with "SAURASHTRA" overprint (1948? without overprint issued in the early 1940's). Also with "U.S.S. REVENUE & POSTAGE SAURASHTRA" overprint used as postage stamp. Without any overprint I've seen 1 a purple, 2 a blue, 4 a light brown and 8 a brown.

BHOPAL


Bhopal, 1880 onwards, 1 a black, with embossing in the center. With year inscribed at the bottom left in Arabic, here 1301 and 1303. Similar fiscal stamps exist with inscription 1297, 1300, 1301, 1303.
Also with inscription "BHOPAL 1 ANNA" 1311 and 1320. Issued from 1880 to 1901.
