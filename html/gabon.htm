Stamps of the French colonies were also used in Gabon, they can be recognized by the cancel "GAB" with dots, example:


A "CORR. D. ARMEES GABON" cancel on a pair of two 5 c French Colonies Ceres stamps.

1889 Postage due stamps of the French colonies surcharged "GABON TIMBRE" and value

  '15' on 5 c black
  '15' on 30 c black
  '25' on 20 c black

This '15' on 5 c and '25' on 20 c provisional issue were made on 27 Mars 1889 (http://www.colfra.org/spip.php?article217), both
in quantities of 1500 stamps.
On 16 April another set of 1500 stamps of the value '25' on 20 c were printed (http://www.colfra.org/spip.php?article218).
On 1 Mai 1889 another batch of 1500 '25' on 20 c stamps were printed together with the '15' on 30 c (250 stamps) (http://www.colfra.org/spip.php?article219).
The overprints were made in three stages, 1) the word "GABON", 2) the word "TIMBRE" and 3) the value.

Gabon stopped issuing its own stamps in 1891 and used the stamps of French Congo instead. Only in 1905 it re-issued its own stamps.
