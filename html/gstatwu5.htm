I have seen postal stationery of 5 p violet, 5 p green, 10 p red and 25 p brown in the same design. I have also seen a postcard with an impression of a 2 p grey, overprinted with three horizontal bars and a further impression of a 3 p brown just next to this 2 p.


25 p 'Postanweisung' postal stationery, I've also seen a 15 p yellow in a similar design

I know that the forger Fournier made a forgery of the 1 M yellow value. Click here for such a Fournier Forgery.


Fournier forgery of the 1 M yellow stamp.


Distinguishing characteristics: left genuine, right Fournier forgery. The Fournier forgery has the oblique line below 'AMT..' all the way to the horizontal line below 'EINE'. In the genuine stamp, this line stops well before this line


Postal forgeries with the stars in front and behind "POST" larger and the lettering rather different. Also no dot behind the words "PFENNIG" or "MARK". According to 'Fakes & Forgeries of Germany + Colonies, No 2, 1966, page 46, these forgeries were made in 1902 by Anton Wolf of Stuttgart. Wolf made at least two more forgeries, possibly different colours of the same values or the crown overprints (next issue).
