Fiscal stamps

Fiscal stamps were issued much earlier (from 1858 onwards) than postal stamps in Ireland.

 

"ADMIRALTY"

Stamps for the admiralty were issued from 1858 to 1881. They appear to be quite rare, I've never seen them.

 

"BANKRUPTCY"

First issued in 1873.

 

"CHANCERY COURT IRELAND FEE FUND"


1867, seal alike stamp. A 1 p and a 6 Sh 2 p exist in this design (both red); in a similar Minerva design exist 2 Pounds, 3 Pounds and 5 Pounds (all in red).


Chancery Court stamps of Great Britain, with "IRELAND FEE FUND" overprint in red. 24 values were overprinted in this manner, ranging from 3 p to 5 Pounds (all in violet)


1868 Queen Victoria, inscription "CHANCERY FEE FUND IRELAND"; the values 2 Pounds lilac, 3 Pounds lilac and 5 Pounds lilac exist.

 

"COUNTY COURTS"


Queen Victoria, I've also seen a 3 p in the same design; same colours. Also larger design: 1 Sh lilac and red.

1878 Queen Victoria, key types of Great Britain

The following values exist:
Small sizes: 1 p lilac and black, 2 p lilac and blue, 3 p lilac and brown, 6 p lilac and green, 8 p lilac and red
Larger size: 1 Sh green and black, 1 Sh 6 p green and blue, 2 Sh green and blue, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and violet, 10 Sh green and red
Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and black
1895 with value surcharged (two types)
Small sizes: 1 p, 2 p, 3 p, 6 p, (all in green and red)
Large size: 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (all in lilac and red)
Even larger size: 1 Pound green and red.


1902: King Edward VII; in the small design exist: 1 p, 2 p, 3 p, 6 p (all in lilac and red); in the slightly larger design: 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh and 10 Sh (all in green and red); a further 1 Pound violet and red exists in an even larger design.


6 p lilac and red, King George V. Also with two lines of Irish overprint.

In the King George V design I have seen (key fiscal types of Great Britain):
1 p, 2 p, 3 p, 6 p; all in lilac and red
Larger size: 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh; all in green and red
Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and red
All values should exist with black instead of red text (I haven't seen them)
With 4 lines of Irish overprint, I have seen: 1 p lilac and red, 2 p lilac and red, 6 p lilac and red, 1 Sh green and red
With 2 lines of Irish overprint: I have seen: 1 p lilac and red, 3 p lilac and red, 6 p lilac and red, 1 Sh green and red, 2 Sh 6 p green and red.

 

"DOG LICENSE"


1865: Dog license, no country name, picture of a dog and text "LICENSE TWO SHILLINGS" violet and 4 sh lilac. Also with "NORTHERN IRELAND" overprint

This dog design was first issued in 1865 (2 Sh lilac, 10 Sh blue and 20 Sh green) and reissued in 1890 with a different watermark.


A single Petty Sessions stamp with Queen Victoria was overprinted "FOR DOG LICENCE" in 1894. Again a single Petty Sessions was overprinted in the same manner in 1903 (now with King Edward VII).


Another Petty Sessions stamp with King George V with "FOR DOG LICENCE" used in 1919. Also a 1 Sh stamp used in 1940 for Northern Ireland.

 

"JUDICATURE"

1878 Queen Victoria, inscription "JUDICATURE IRELAND"

In this design were issued: 1 p lilac and black, 3 p lilac and brown, 4 p lilac and violet (1882), 6 p lilac and green
Larger design: 1 Sh green and black, 1 Sh 6 p green and blue, 2 Sh green and blue, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and lilac, 10 Sh green and red
Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and black, 5 Pounds lilac and green.

 

1896 Queen Victoria with value printed on the stamps

In this design were issued: 1 p, 3 p, 4 p, 6 p (all in green and red); 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (all in lilac and red); 1 Pound, 5 Pound (both in green and red)


1880 Judicature stamp of Great Britain, overpritned "IRELAND"; only the 4 p lilac was issued.

1903 King Edward VII

In this design were issued: 1 p, 3 p, 4 p, 6 p (all in lilac and red); 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (all in green and red); 1 Pound, 5 Pound (both in lilac and red)

 

LAND COMMISSION IRELAND"

1881 Queen Victoria


Land Commission 1 Sh green and black. Also 1 Sh black and blue (with value printed ont he stamp)

The following values were issued (in the key fiscal Queen Victoria design): 2 p lilac and blue, 3 p lilac and brown, 6 p lilac and green. In a larger design: 1 Sh green and black, 2 Sh green and blue, 5 Sh green and violet.
In 1895 similar stamps were issued with the value printed on the stamp: 6 p lilac and blue, 1 Sh green and blue.

In 1902 stamps with King Edward VII were issued: 6 p lilac and blue, 1 Sh green and blue.

 

"LAND REGISTRY"

In 1906, 8 stamps were issued with the image of King Edward VII: 3 p, 4 p, 6 p (all in lilac and red), 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (all in green and red), 1 Pound lilac and red.

 

"PETTY SESSIONS"


1858 seal alike design with "IRELAND PETTY SESSIONS" overprint, several dates exist (the numbers in the design); only 6 p green and 6 p red were issued.


6 p red Queen Victoria and 6 p green, also with "FOR DOG LICENCE" overprint

In this 1865 Queen Victoria design were issued: 6 p red, 6 p green, 6 p blue (rare) and 2 Sh 6 p lilac (slightly different design, exist in two shades; lilac or violet).

 

1902 King Edward VII, key types of the fiscal stamps of Great Britain

6 p red and green, 6 p green and olive, 2 Sh 6 p lilac and olive (larger size)
With 2 lines of Irish overprint: 2 Sh 6 p lilac and olive (1922)


6 p green King Edward VII with "FOR DOG LICENCE" overprint

1912 King George V, key types of the fiscal stamps of Great Britain


6 p red, King George V with 2 lines of Irish overprint and "1922"

I have seen:
6 p red and olive, 6 p green and olive
With overprint in 4 lines of Irish text: 6 p red and olive, 6 p green,
With 2 lines of Irish overprint: 6 p green, 6 p red and green
With 2 lines of Irish overprint and "1922": 6 p green, 6 p red and green


6 p green, King George V with "FOR DOG LICENCE" overprint. Also a 1 Sh green King George V with "PETTY SESSIONS NORTHERN IRELAND" and "FOR DOG LICENCE" overprint.

 

"POLICY MARINE INSURANCE"

 

"RECORDS"

1893 Queen Victoria, inscription "RECORDS IRELAND"

6 p lilac and green
Larger size: 1 Sh green and black, 5 Sh green and violet, 10 Sh green and red
Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and black
With overprint in numbers; 1 Sh green and brown

1902 King Edward VII

6 p lilac and green, 1 Sh green and brown, 5 Sh green and brown, 1 Pound green and brown.

 

"RECORDS OF TITLES FEE"

As Registration of Deeds Fee stamps, but with inscription "RECORDS OF TITTLES FEE": 1 Sh green, 2 Sh 6 p green (both rare).

 

"REGISTRATION OF DEEDS FEE"


1861 Queen Victoria issue; 3 p, 4 p, 6 p, 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p (image above), 8 Sh, 10 Sh, 1 Pound (all in redbrown color).


1902 Greyish blue design, inscription "IRELAND" (here with red seal cancel): 3 p, 4 p, 6 p, 9 p, 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 8 Sh, 10 Sh, 1 Pound (all different design, but all with platenumber A).

 

1902 With image of Kind Edward VII, key fiscal type of Great Britain with inscription "IRELAND REGISTRATION OF DEEDS"

Small size: 3 p, 4 p, 6 p; all in lilac and green
Larger size: 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh; all in green
Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and green.

 

"REGISTRATION OF TITLE"

1890 Queen Victoria: 3 p lilac and brown, 4 p lilac and violet, 6 p lilac and green. Larger size: 1 Sh green and black, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and violet, 10 Sh green and red. Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac. In 1895 the 1 Sh was reissued but now with the value printed on the stamp: 1 Sh green and lilac.

1902 King Edward VII: small size: 3 p, 4 p, 6 p (all in lilac); larger size: 1 Sh green (2 types), 2 Sh 6 p green and lilac, 5 Sh green and lilac, 10 Sh green and lilac, 1 Pound green and lilac.

 

"REGISTRATION OF TITLE INSURANCE FUND"


1890: Queen Victoria; smaller size 2 Sh green and blue, 10 Sh green and red. Larger size 1 Pound lilac and black. Also with value printed on the stamp: 2 Sh green and lilac.

With the image of King Edward VII appeared in 1902: 1 Sh green and brown, 2 Sh green and black, 10 Sh green and black.
