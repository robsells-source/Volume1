Fiscal stamps

Many fiscal stamps were issued for Great Britain. I hereby give a list of the different purposes: admirality, bankruptcy court, board of agriculture, chancery court, civil service, common law courts, companies registration, companines winding up, consular service, contact note, copyhold, customs, district audit, foreign bill, hair powder, in chancery, judicature fees, land commission, land registry, life policy, match tax, matrimonial cuase fee, medicine labels, mixture for coffee, naturalization, patent, paymaster general's service, perfume duty, pedlars' certificate, police courts, probate courts, public records, sea policies, transfer duty.

Literature: The Forbin Guide (1915, in French)
Revenue Catalogue, part 1 Great Britain and Colonies by Fred. Geo. C. Lundy (1891).

Great Britain Fiscal stamps, Consular Service

"CONTRACT NOTE"

One of the more common fiscal stamps of Great Britain (according to the price given in the Forbin Guide).


Contract Note: Queen Victoria

The values 6 p (smaller size), 1 Sh, 1 Sh 6 p, 2 Sh, 4 Sh, 5 Sh and 10 Sh exist (all in the colours lilac and black).


Contract Note, King Edward VII

With the image of King Edward VII: 3 p lilac and black, 6 p lilac and black, 6 p red and black; Larger size: 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 3 Sh, 4 Sh, 5 Sh, 6 Sh, 8 Sh, 10 Sh (all in lilac and black, 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 4 Sh and 10 Sh also in lilac and brown). Even larger size: 1 Pound lilac and red.
Some values were re-issued in 1915 in more reddish colors?


Contract Note: King George V

1921 With the image of King George V: 3 p lillac and black, 6 p lilac and black. Larger size: 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 3 Sh, 4 Sh, 5 Sh, 6 Sh, 8 Sh, 10 Sh (all in lilac and black). Even larger size 1 Pound lilac and red.
Exist with overprint "Saorstat eireann" for Ireland.


Contract Note: King George VI

With the image of King George VI, 6 p lilac and blue. Larger size: 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 3 Sh, 4 Sh, 6 Sh, 8 Sh, 10 Sh (all in lilac and black). Even larger size: 1 Pounds lilac and red, 2 Pounds lilac and red text.


Contract Note: Queen Elizabeth II

1959: With the image of Queen Elizabeth II, I have seen: 6 p lilac and black. Larger size: 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 3 Sh, 4 Sh, 6 Sh, 8 Sh, 10 Sh (all in lilac and black). Even larger size: 1 Pound, 2 Pounds (both in lilac and red).


10 p with large and small "P", "CONTRACT NOTE" in two lines or in one line

"COPYHOLD & COMMISSION"


1 Pound red with blue text "COPYHOLD & COMMISSION"

1868 Seal alike stamp with crown and overprint "COPYHOLD & COMMISSION" in blue with various datestamps: 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh, 1 Pound, 5 Pounds (all in red color). Issued imperforate or perforated (rare).

1873-1881 Queen Victoria (all quite rare).
1873: 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (all in green and red color); Larger size: 1 Pound, 5 Pounds (both in violet and green color)
1877 Color change: 5 Sh green and lilac, 1 Pound violet and black
1881 Another color change: 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and violet, 10 Sh green and red, 1 Pound lilac and black, 1 Pound lilac and green

These stamps were replaced by Land Commission fiscal stamps in 1882.

 

"CUSTOMS"


1856 issue, pink imperforate with black "CUSTOMS" overprint and black border. 1 p, plate B, datestamp 6-4-60. 4 p, plate A datetstamp 31-5-60. 6 p with datestamp 2-4-60 and letter U (cut to shape). 1 Sh, plate F datestamp 16-6-60. 5 Sh with datestamp 9-4-60 and letter F in the design, 10 Sh plate H datestamp 26-4-60. And 1 Pound datestamp 17-5-60, plate A.


1882 issue, Royal Crown; 8 p, plate D, datestamp 7-11-00. 3 Sh 9 p, plate E, datestamp 21-10-86. 5 Sh, plate I, platenumber 21-10-86. 1 Sh 3 p, plate O, datestamp 25-4-99.


1889 Imperial Crown: 3 p red, datestamp 13-6-95 with letter N in the upper left side. 2 Sh 6 p, plate Q, royal crown, datestamp 6-11-84. 2 Sh 6 p, plate V, imperial crown, datestamp 8-11-00. 3 Sh 9 p, plate F, datestamp 18-4-96. 5 Sh, plate N, datestamp 17-1-89.

In the above 'seal' design many fiscal stamps were issued from 1856 to 1904 when the use of Custom stamps was discontinued. They were initially issued in the color pink and then in the colour red. The date is indicated in the seal as a datestamp. In 1871 they first appeared with perforation. The word "CUSTOMS" was actually printed first and then the embossed design printed on top of that.

1856 Pink color "CUSTOMS" overprint in black: 1 p (plate A or B), 4 p (plate A), 6 p (plate T or U), 1 Sh (plate E or F), 2 Sh (plate K), 5 Sh (plate F), 10 Sh (plate H or I), 1 Pound (plate A), 5 Pounds (plate G), 10 Pounds (plate D).
1871 Pink color "CUSTOMS" in thin blue letters, perforated: 8 p (plate B), 1 Sh 3 p (plate O), 2 Sh 6 p (plate N or O), 3 Sh 9 p (plate C), 5 Sh (plate G)
1875 Red color "CUSTOMS" in thin blue letters, perforated: 8 p (plate E), 1 Sh 3 p (plate O), 2 Sh 6 p (plate O), 3 Sh 9 p (plate D), 5 Sh (plate G)
1882 Red color "CUSTOMS" in thin blue letters, perforated, Royal crown: 3 p (plate E, I), 8 p (plate B, D or E), 1 Sh 3 p (plate N, O or P), 2 Sh 6 p (plate O or Q), 3 Sh 9 p (plate C or E), 5 Sh (plate G or I)
1889 Red color "CUSTOMS" in thin blue letters, perforated, Imperial crown: 3 p (plate J, L or N), 2 Sh 6 p (plate V), 3 Sh 9 p (plate F), 5 Sh (plate N).
1901 Red color "CUSTOMS" in thin bue letters, perforated, 3 p (plate F), 8 p (different design: plate D, 1 Sh 3 p (different design: plate C), 2 Sh 6 p (plate I), 5 Sh (different design; plate H or J).


1901 issue: 3 p red (datestamp 11-9-01, plate F). 8 p plate D, datestamp 8-8-04. 1 Sh 3 p, plate C, datestamp 14-6-01. 5 Sh, plate H, datestamp 16-10-01.

In 1860 a few stamps with the image of Queen Victoria and the inscription "CUSTOMS" were issued. The following values exist (all different frames): 1 p red, 4 p red, 6 p red, 1 Sh lilac, 1 Sh 6 p red (also redbrown or red on blue), 2 Sh lilac, 5 Sh lilac, 10 Sh lilac, 1 Pound green, 5 Pounds green (larger size) and 10 Pounds green (larger size).

The use of Customs fiscal stamps was discontinued at the end of 1904.

"EXCISE REVENUE"

Issued from 1916 onwards, crown in the center. More than 100 different stamps were issued.

Similar stamps exist for Northern Ireland, inscription "NORTHERN IRELAND" on top, "EXCISE REVENUE" at the bottom.
