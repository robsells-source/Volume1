Fiscal stamps

Many fiscal stamps were issued for Great Britain. I hereby give a list of the different purposes: admirality, bankruptcy court, board of agriculture, chancery court, civil service, common law courts, companies registration, companines winding up, consular service, contact note, copyhold, customs, district audit, foreign bill, hair powder, in chancery, judicature fees, land commission, land registry, life policy, match tax, matrimonial cuase fee, medicine labels, mixture for coffee, naturalization, patent, paymaster general's service, perfume duty, pedlars' certificate, police courts, probate courts, public records, sea policies, transfer duty.

Literature: The Forbin Guide (1915, in French)
Revenue Catalogue, part 1 Great Britain and Colonies by Fred. Geo. C. Lundy (1891).

Great Britain Fiscal stamps, Admiralty Court to Companies Winding Up

"CONSULAR SERVICE"

1885 Seal alike design

For use in Europe, Africa and the Americas:


1885 issue; seal alike design. 5 Sh with value inscribed once and 1 Pound value inscribed twice.

The following values exist in the above seal design: 6 p blue, 1 Sh red, 2 Sh red, 2 Sh 6 p red, 5 Sh red, 10 Sh red, 1 Pound red, 1 Pound violet. The 1 Sh, 5 Sh and 10 Sh exist with value inscribed once or twice. The 6 p and 1 Sh also exist with small or large lettering.

For use in Asia (overprinted in Dollars or Cents):


50 cts "HALF DOLLAR" blue seal design; here used in the British Consular Service in Chefoo. Also $1 1/2 "ONE DOLLAR & A HALF" in red.

The following values exist in the above seal design:
25 c blue, 50 c blue, 75 c blue (two types of "75"), 1 $ red, 1 1/2 $ red (4 types), 2 $ red, 3 $ red, 5 $ violet, 6 $ violet and 20 $ violet.

 

1886 Queen Victoria design;

For use in Europe, Africa and the Americas:


Queen Victoria 1886 issue;

In the above 1886 design exist: 1 p lilac and black, 2 p lilac and blue, 3 p lilac and brown, 4 p lilac and violet, 6 p lilac and green
In a larger design: 1 Sh green and black, 2 Sh green and blue, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and violet and 10 Sh green and red
In an even larger design: 1 Pound lilac and black, 2 Pounds lilac and blue, 3 Pounds lilac and brown, 5 Pounds lilac and green.

Queen Victoria, 1887 issue.
