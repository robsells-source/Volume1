Nossi-Be, 1889 issues

 

1890 Stamps of the French colonies, surcharged "NSB" and value:

  'NSB 25 c.' on 20 c red on green
  'NSB 25 c.' and border on 20 c red on green
  'NSB 25 c.' on 75 c red
  'NSB 25 c.' and border on 75 c red
  'NSB 25 c.' on 1 F green
  'NSB 25 c.' and border on 1 F green
  'NSB 0 25' on 20 c red on green
  'NSB 0 25' on 75 c red
  'NSB 0 25' on 1 F green

Value of the stamps

 
vc = very common
c  = common
*  = not so common
** = uncommon
 
*** = very uncommon
R   = rare
RR  = very rare
RRR = extremely rare

The forger Fournier made forgeries of these stamps.

Forged "NOSSI-BE 10 OCT 91 MADAGASCAR" cancel (second row, last cancel).


This might be a Fournier forged overprint on a genuine stamp. Note the short right leg of the "N", the very open "S" and the closed corners of the rectangles. Next to it forged overprints on forged stamps some with "FAUX" overprint.


Closed corners of the rectangles, dubious item?


Handmade(?) primitive forgery.


Another forgery, the stamp, overprint and cancel are forged. Note that in this forgery the "O" and the "N" of "COLONIES" are further apart than in the genuine stamps. Also note the strange "E" of "FRANCAISE". The perforation does not match in the corners. I believe these forgeries were made by the stamp forger Oneglia.
