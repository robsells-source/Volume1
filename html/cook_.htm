Cancels:

The cancel "COOK ISLANDS P.O. RAROTONGA" in a circle (see first two images above) seems to be the most common. From 1893 to 1896 the following cancel was used (third image): "RAROTONGA" in a straight line with the date above or below, the whole in a single circle (RAROTONGA below the date is rare and only used from May to July 1893). I've also seen "POST OFFICE COOK ISLANDS" and date in a box (see 10 p image above) and several mute cancels. I don't know if the "RAROTONGA" in between 7 bars is genuine.


A dubious "N.T." cancel that I have also seen applied on forgeries of Transvaal and Natal.

Forgeries:

Example of a Fournier forgery:


Fournier forgeries with cancel "REGISTERED 2 SP 92 PALMERSTON". I've also seen the 10 p value with this cancel. Apparently Fournier also used this cancel on forgeries from Fiji (see last image).

The forger Fournier offers deceptive forgeries of these stamps in his 1914 pricelist. He offers 3 values only (1 p, 2 1/2 p and 10 p) for 1.50 Swiss Francs. Apparently Fournier did not forge the 1 1/2 p value. The Fournier forgeries are too small. In 'The Fournier Album of Philatelic Forgeries' these stamps can be found cancelled with the above registration cancel "REGISTERED 2 SP 92 PALMERSTON" or a numeral cancel "12" with dots at the sides and two lines above and below the "12". I've also seen "EGISTERED" in a straight line (probably part of "REGISTERED"). Uncancelled and imperforate Fournier forgeries can also be found in this album. Apparently, Fournier did not make any forgeries of the 1 1/2 p value.


Two Fournier forgeries. The first numeral cancel "12" can be found in the Fournier Album (see last image, I've also seen it on a Samoa forgery), the 10 p bears the cancel "REGISTERED 2 SP 92 PALMERSTON" once more. I've seen the numeral "12" cancel on a forgery of the first issue of India as well.


Fournier forgeries of the 2 1/2 , 1 p and 10 p value as they can be found in 'The Fourier Album of Philatelic Forgeries'


Another set from a Fournier album.


A Fournier set with "COOK ISLANDS 5 MAY 189? POSTAGE" cancel, an unreadable cancel and a "NAUSORIPOR 5 ?? FIJI" cancel.


Distinguishing characteristics for the 10 p (dot in front of 'E') and 2 1/2 p Fournier forgeries (line in lower right corner).


A 'REGISTERED' cancel taken from a Fournier Album (in the 'OBLITERATIONS DIVERSES' section); this cancel was probably used to cancel some of the above Fournier forgeries.

Distinguishing characteristics of the Fournier forgeries (according to the Serrane guide):
1 Penny: The "Y" of "PENNY" has the right hand top part too short. The "N" of "FEDERATION" also has a too short right hand leg at the top. There is a small dot in the lower part of the "R" of this word.
2 1/2 Pence: the lower right corner has a blue vertical line in the ornaments that is not present in the genuine stamps.
10 Pence: There is a small dot in front of the "E" of "POSTAGE".



Apparently the 1 p was printed in sets of 3, while the 10 p was printed alone.

I've seen an imperforate 1 p stamp, is this a forgery?:


Imperforate forgery, this forgery seems to have been cut from a Maury catalogue (next image).

I've been told that the next forgery has a watermark 'Script Crown CA' (which was never used on the genuine stamps; they have no watermark!). The watermark is larger than the genuine 'Script Crown CA' watermark.


(Forgery with watermark)


The same forgery as shown above, but now uncancelled. The outer frameline appears to be too thin and the lettering is slightly different; "K" of "COOK" etc. The "P" of "POSTAGE" has a very squeezed top part.


Another forgery (I've been told from Fournier, but it doesn't resemble the above forgery). It has the "2" in the "1/2" different. The "S"s of "ISLANDS" are more open. The word "POSTAGE" is thinner. A similar 1 p forgery is shown on http://www.numonesidentifier.com/country/35/ (website no longer availalbe...), where the same "APIA ? - 6N." cancel is used (Apia is situated in Samoa!). Both forgeries have a very thin outer frameline. The outer framelines also do not exhibit the typical breaks of the genuine stamps. Also note the "P" of "POSTAGE" which is similar to the 10 p forgeries shown above.


And all values of the above forgery set. These stamps do not appear to have a watermark. Also note that the letters in "POSTAGE" are thinner than in the genuine stamps. I think these might be Oneglia forgeries


Futher proof that these forgeries were made by Oneglia, the 1 p forgery has a bars with "1" cancel and the 2 1/2 p has a bars with "G" cancel.


Another highly dubious item, the perforation is slanting and not far away enough from the design.


Looks like another forgery to me.

From 1932 on new stamps with inscription "COOK ISLANDS" were issued again. The stamps of Aitutaki, Penrhyn and Rarotonga stopped issuing stamps, while Niue continued to issue its own stamps.

 

1932 Inscription "COOK ISLANDS"

1/2 p green and black (captain Cook landing), 1 p red and black (portrait of Cook), 2 p brown and black (local ship), 2 1/2 p blue and black (hut at the beach), 4 p blue and black (island view), 6 p orange and black (steamer), 1 Sh violet and black (King George V)

Overprinted "SILVER JUBILEE OF KING GEORGE V 1910 1935": 1 p red and brown, 2 1/2 p blue (red overprint), 6 p orange and green.

Stamps with inverted center are most likely printer's waste.

 

1936 Stamps of New Zealand (admirals), overprinted "COOK ISLANDS"
