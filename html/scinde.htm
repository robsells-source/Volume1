1852 Seal of the Scinde District Dawk

  1/2 a red embossed
  1/2 a white (embossed)
  1/2 a blue (with extra outer ring in the design)

All the above shown stamps are genuine. The red stamp was issued first and was probably printed individually with red sealing wax. It cracked too easily and was replaced by the embossed stamp. However, this was badly visible and the blue stamp was introduced. However, the blue stamp was only used for a limited period of time. The letter "E I C" stand for East India Company. These stamps were the first stamps to be issued in India. 12 different kinds of postmarks are known; see https://www.indianpostagestamps.com/scinde/postmark.html among them a dot cancel with 7x7 dots (corner ones larger), an 8x8 dotted cancel, a 9x9 dotted cancel, a dotted round large cancel, a dotted round small cancel, two kinds of sunburst cancels, 7 parallel lines, numeral "96" cancel (Karachi?), etc. These stamps were in use from 1st July 1852 to 30 September 1854. The "4" in the design actually also appears on coins of the period and is part of the arms (or merchant mark) of the East India Company; see https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India_Company.

Examples of forgeries:


The above forgeries have the coloured outer part too big and inscription 'ETC' instead of 'EIC'. The "4" is not broad enough.


This forgery has the buckle completely different from a genuine stamp (it doesn't even touch the white line above it). This might be the forgery "a)" described in the Serrane guide. All three varieties are printed on one sheet.



These forgeries have the "4" much thinner than in the genuine stamps. I've also seen the blue stamp without cancel. The first "A" of "ANNA" is too far away from the "S" of "SCINDE".
