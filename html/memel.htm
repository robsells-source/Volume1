Forgeries:
In the American Philatelist, Vol. 36 (7), April 1923, page 353; the following text can be found:
MEMEL
The February tenth number of Stamp Collecting calls attention to forgeries of Memel stamps. The entire notice is worth quoting. It reads as follows: COUNTERFEIT STAMPS OF MEMEL.
Our special correspondent at Libau, Latvia, Mr. George H. Jaeger, informs us that on January 3rd, Kurt Henning, stamp dealer and president of the Memel Postage Stamp Dealer's Association, his wife, and also a printer, named Paul Diessner, were arrested by the police of Memel. They were accused of the manufacture of counterfeit rarities of Memel.
Mr. Henning confessed that he had made the forgeries with the assistance of the printer, Paul Diessner, of Konigsberg. Only rare specimens were forged. So far as we know at present, the forgeries comprise single values of the following airpost stamps, Types I and II:-—
"Flugpost," Type I:
60pf., small "p.," ord. o'pt.
60pf., small "p.," inverted.
60pf., large "p.," ord. o'pt.
2m., inverted.
3m., inverted.
"Flugpost," Type II:
3m. vertical, ord. o'pt.
3m. vertical, inverted.
9m., inverted.
Henning had paid to Diessner 1,000m. for each forged stamp. The face value of the forgeries amounts to millions. The owners of the above-mentioned stamps will soon find that they are forgeries, for such varieties were never issued. All stamp dealers who have been in business relations with Kurt Henning, of Memel, would be well advised to examine their stocks of Memel postage stamps.

(Examples of stamps of Memel)

 

Fiscal Stamps

1921 Inscription "MEMELGEBIET STEUERMARKE", value in black

The following values exist: 1/2 M orange, 1 M red, 2 M orange, 5 M green, 10 M grey, 20 M red, 50 M violet, 100 M blue, 500 M brown. The values 500 M red, 2000 M lilac, 3000 M olive and 5000 M violet should also exist, but I have not seen them.

 

1923(?) Inscription "KLAIPEDOS KRASTAS MEMELGEBIET WECHSELSTEMPEL" (Bill of Exchange)

In the above design I have seen the values: 25 c, 50 c, 1 L and 2 L. I have still seen them being used as late as 1930.

 

Inscription "KLAIPEDA" on top, "MEMELGEBIET" at the bottom

In this design I have seen:
a) Without overprint: 2 L grey, 4 L light brown, 5 L green, 6 L orange, 10 L red, 20 L blue
b) 1925: With "Wechl.-St." overprint (Wechsel Stempel = Bill of Exchange):: 50 c red, 1 L blue and 2 L grey; "0,50" on 1 L blue
c) 1923: With "Fr. U. St." overprint (Fracht Urkunden Stempel = bill of lading): 3 L lilac, 6 L orange
d) 1925: With "Kat. G" (Kadaster = Land Registry)


Different text on top and arms changed: 100 L green

 

Arms of Memel, inscription "MEMELGEBIET" in an arch on top, value printed in black

In this design I have seen (the value inscription is always in black):
a) With inscription "Staats-Stempel": 15 Pf, 30 Pf, 2 M, 3 M, 4 M, 5 M, 10 M, 25 M, 50 M, 100 M, 200 M (see image above), 300 M, 500 M; all in green
b) With inscription "Staats-Stempel" and overprint "S.V.A.": 5 Pf, 15 Pf, 30 Pf, 50 Pf, 1 M, 1 1/2 M, 2 M, 2 1/2 M, 3 M, 20 M; all in green. I have also seen "1 Lit." (red) and two red lines on 30 p green (see image above).
c) With inscription "S.V.A." (Staats Verkehrs Anstalt) at the bottom: 20 p blue, 2 M blue, 10 M blue, 15 M blue and 20 M blue.
