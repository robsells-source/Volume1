MYSORE


Government of Mysore, 1 a black, issued in 1887. A 1 a orange also exists (probably the 1890 issue mentioned in the Forbin Guide). .


Mysore 1874 "TRANSFER STAMP" and 1874 "RECEIPT BILL OR DRAFT". Also a red stamp with "APPLICATION STAMP" printed on it; unlisted in Forbin.


Mysore: Foreign Bill stamp of India, surcharged "MYSORE", issued in 1874. 15 values were overprinted. Also a 1 a revenue stamp with this overprint and a Court Fee stamp.

A vertical overprint "MYSORE" also exists on the embossed stamps with green "FOREIGN BILL" of 1860; see http://www.indiastaterevenues.com/Templates/Mysore.htm.


Later issues, also with "C.&M.Stn." (Civil and Military Station). I've seen it with "C & M.St". I've also seen a "Half Anna" on 1 a surcharged stamp. Also a similar design with "MYSORE ENTERTAINMENT TAX".


"GOVERNMENT OF MYSORE RECEIPT": 1 a lilac, issued around 1950.


Court Fee stamp of India of 1957 with "MYSORE" overprint. I've also seen 25 np.

NARSINGARH STATE

NAWANAGAR


Fiscal stamp of Nawanagar of 1887 with Indian inscriptions only. Printed in sheets of 4.


A 1890 fiscal stamp with inscription "NAWANAGAR STATE" in green. I've seen it with a black blob of ink on the lower left corner (to change the value?).

The Forbin Guide lists a bogus stamp of 6 pies red, with the image of a lion and a palm tree. An image can be found in Walter Morley's book 'Catalogue of the Revenue Stamps of the British Colonies' (1910).

Court Fee stamps also exist of Nawanagar. They usually have the year written in the design, i.e. "1925-26" etc.


"1944-45", here with additional "ATTACHED AREA" overprint, furthermore with perforation "NAWANAGAR STATE"

PATDI


1931 to 1945: Court Fees. The value inscription is added by hand in the left panel (i.e. number of Rupees, then number of annas and then number of pies); 1 a, 2 a, 4 a (image on the right), 8 a, 1 R, 3 R, 5 R. The color of the stamps always seems to be violet (handstamped). Usually cancelled with a small hole or manuscript cancel. .

PATIALA STATE


Arms of Patiala; 1 a brown. Listed in the Forbin Guide and issued in 1906.


Fiscal stamp of India of 1936 with "PATIALA STATE" overprint. I've also seen the 1 a lilac with this overprint.

PATNA


1906(?) "PATNA COURT FEES" violet in a circle. The value was added by hand in "RUPEES" and "ANNAS". Not listed in Walter Morley though....


1914(?) issue handstamped in violet.


"RECEIPT"


Revenue stamp of India of 1934, overprinted 'PATNA STATE" and "Add 12 1/2 % additional duty". It also exists with "PATNA STATE" overprint alone.

I've seen court fee stamps of British India with the image of King George V with a two-lined overprint "PATNA STATE": 4 a.

I've also seen court fee stamps of British India with King George VI with "PATNA STATE" in two lines: 4 a.

PEINT STATE

Peint State overprinted some values of the Court Fee stamps of Brisith India of 1872 with a blue "PEINT STATE" overprint or a black or red "Peint State" overprint (see Forbin Guide). |
Also a Receipt stamp of British India (1 a lilac) was overprinted with a red "PEINT STATE".
