ATTENTION: many of the local stamps of the United States are reprints or forgeries! I have never seen an area with more forgeries, reprints and even bogus issues than the locals and carriers of the United States in even non-existing colors. If I do not specifically state that an item is genuine in this catalogue, then I'm not sure that it is!

Specialist distinguish between postmaster issues (official issues before the general issues of the United States in1847), locals, carriers, express company labels etc. A usefull introduction on locals, carriers etc. can be found on: http://www.pennypost.org/Collecting%20US%20Locals%20by%20Hahn%201976%20Updated.pdf.

The most common locals are those of Blood's, Boyd's, Hussey's and Swart's. Most others are rare to extremely rare. I have decided to list these stamps anyway (even if only a few copies are known), so collectors can be warned against the many forgeries of these stamps.

Sanitary fair stamps and Bazaar post

Sanitairy fair stamps are unofficial stamps issued by the Sanitary Fair Commission in order to raise funds for the Union troops (sometimes these stamps are considered as a forerunner of a red cross issue). The first sanitary fair stamp was issued in 1863 with inscription 'Young Ladies of Brooklyn Bazaar'. Examples:


Images obtained from a Rumsey auction

The above stamp was issued in 1864 for a army relief bazaar in Albany, New York. A 10 c red and 10 c black exist. I have seen forgeries in the colours blue, black, green and red.

Stamps with inscription Tabernacle Fair were made by the forger Taylor.


(Left image obtained from a Siegel auction)

Some other bazaar stamps with inscription "Bazaar Post Office" stamp were issued in 1864 with an eagle in the design (value 10 c; colours blue, red or black). See image above.

Again another design with an eagle with the text "CENTRAL FAIR POSTAGE SANITARY COMMISION" was also issued in 1864 (values 10 c blue, 20 c green and 30 c black); see image above.
Be careful when acquiring cancelled stamps (these stamps are worth about 10 times more in cancelled condition). Even quite recently (October 2002), unused stamps were for sale on E-bay, to show up one month later in cancelled condtion on the same internet auction platform ! (source: http://www.pcug.org.au/~sheryll/Forgeries/Fraud/Forgeries_article_Fraud.htm ). Reprints and proofs seem to exist in relative large quantities (also in bogus colors; source Scott catalogue).

Army Frank Stamps

The above stamps with inscription "ARMY FRANK OFFICIAL BUSINESS ONLY", are bogus issues. They were made in 1898 by a certain Major Brewster Cox Kenyon (1864-?) and clearly inspired by the 30 c USA eagle stamp of 1869. Remainders were sold in 1903. They only exist in the colours blue, red and brown (as above shown). This person also made the bogus Central American Steamship Co stamps. Imperforate stamps seem to exist, but are much rarer.
