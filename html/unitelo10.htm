TEESE & Co PENNY POST

Teese&Co Penny Post Philad'a

Only a blue stamp was issued in 1852. Very similar stamps were issued for Blood's. They are not very rare. I know that Hussey, Scott and Taylor made forgeries of this stamp. I do not know the distinguishing characteristics of the genuine stamps.

UNION DESPATCH

Union Despatch (Chicago)


(Genuine, images obtained from a Siegel auction)


(Genuine, images obtained from a Siegel auction)

Though not listed in most catalogues, the Union Despatch stamp seems to have existed genuinly in Chicago. Two values exist: 5 c red and 20 c green. According to a Siegel auction, the genuine design has faint vertical lines in the vignette background around the head of the horse. Genuine stamps also show at least a part of the rouletted perforation. Only six 5 c red stamps and two 20 c green stamps are known to exist.

UNION SQUARE P.O.

Union Square P.O. New York

Issued in 1852 in 1 c (black on green) and 2 c (black on lilac). These stamps are not very rare unused. Forgeries exist.

Forgeries:

First forgery of the 1 c value:

In this forgery the "R" in "SQUARE" is replaced by a "H". Also, the "C" and "I" are touching at the bottom, the "C"is also different from a genuine stamp. It exists in bogus colors, for example black on blue (see image above). I've also seen a block of 6 printed in tete-beche (3 on top, 3 inverted at the bottom).

Second forgery of the 1 c value

In this forgery, the "U" of "UNION" is touching the left frame line. The "Y" of "CITY" is placed at some distance from "CIT". It exists in several bogus colors as well: black on lilac, black on grey. The bottom line of the "Q" is also placed too low.

Third forgery of the 1 c value

This forgery is rather deceptive. In my view, the central part of the "M" of of "MAIL" does not go down far enough. The border line is not broken on top. This might be the forgery made by Scott.

Fourth forgery of the 1 c value

The fourth forgery has the "C" of "CITY" very close to the frameline below it. I have seen it in many colors: black on lilac, red, black, blue, orange, black on violet, grey, red on yellow, black on yellow, red on blue, etc. Presumably this forgery was made by the stamp forger Allan Taylor.

Other forgeries might exist of the 1 c value.

First forgery of the 2 c value:

This forgery has the "C" of "CITY" too small.

Second forgery of the 2 c value:

There is a very fat dot at the bottom part of the stamp (when compared to the genuine stamp). The "D" of "DISPATCH" is too small and the "S" of "SQUARE" is different from the genuine 'S'. If my information is correct, this forgery was made by the forger Taylor. I've also seen it in the color black on green (the color of the 1 c).

Third forgery of the 2 c value:

In my opinion, the "C" of "CITY" has a too pronounced upwards pointing right bottom part. Also the dot between the "P" and "O" is placed slightly too far to the left when compared to the dot just below it. If my information is correct, this forgery might have been produced by Scott.

Other forgeries of the 2 c value exist.

WALTON & CO

Walton&Co's City Express Post (Brooklyn, 1846)


Genuine, first image obtained from http://www.siegelauctions.com/1999/817/yf817278.htm

Walton & Co issued only one stamp: the 2 c black on lilac. The only cancel I have seen is "PAID W.W." in two lines. I have also seen these stamps cut to an oval shape on letter.


(A bogus Walton & Co stamp in a completely different design, apparently made by the forger S. Allan Taylor. I have also seen the 2 c red and 2 c black on yellow values). He also used the frame to make 'Blockade' bogus stamps.

WASHINGTON DESPATCH

Washington Despatch (Washington and Baltimore)

1856 Delivery rider on a horse with letter in his hands


Right stamp obtained from a Kelleher auction


Issue for Baltimore, two types exist; one with the letter poiting to the "O" and one to the "N" of "ONE"


(images obtained from a Siegel auction)

  1 c violet (inscription "WASHINGTON CITY" at bottom)
  1 c red (no name at bottom)

This local post was founded by Mr. Wiley. There are two types of the 1 c red; in type I the letter is pointing towards the "O" of "ONE", in type II this message points to the "N" of this same word.
