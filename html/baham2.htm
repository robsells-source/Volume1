The forger Sperati made a forgery of the 1 Pound value by erasing the design of a 1 p stamp (the BPA handbook says a 4 p stamp...) and printing a new value on it.


Sperati forgery, the differences with a genuine stamp are microscopic. If I'm well informed, there is a green spot of color in the white line to the right of the head of the King (at the level where the shell touches its oval containment).

Postal stationery

In 1881 an envelope was issued with the portrait of Queen Victoria in an ellipse (4 p violet). In 1891 a surcharge was applied '2 1/2 ' on 4 p violet in red or black in several types. An envelope of 2 1/2 p blue was issued in 1892. In 1894 a registered envelope with value 2 p blue was issued.

A postcard was issued in 1881 (Queen Victoria, 1 1/2 p red). This postcard was surcharged 1 p in 1892. A new postcard in the value 1 p red was issued in 1892.
