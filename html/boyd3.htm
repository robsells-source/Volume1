USA Locals Boyd's issues, eagle issues, part 1

14th issue:


14th issue (1857).


15th issue (1860), made from the 14th issue by changing "2" into "1" and removing the "S" of "CENTS", but parts of this letter are still visible in many stamps. Here some remainders (huge amounts of remainders exist, while used stamps are quite rare). I've seen a whole sheet of 100 stamps (10 rows of 10).


16th issue (1861). The same printing stone as that of the 11th to 13th issue was used. These exists in tete-beche, since the top row was printed inverted in the sheet.


Scott forgeries of the 16th issue


17th issue (1861), it also exists as a misprint with "CENT" instead of "CENTS" (one position of the sheet). Also an error with an inverted "1" exists. These stamps were made from the 16th issue (by correcting the inverted stamps, such that tete-beche stamps don't exist).


18th issue (1861), it is the same as the 17th issue, but the 17th issue is printed in black on lilac, while the 18th issue is printed in black on blue grey.


1861: 19th issue in gold color. 20th issue gold on green.


The next few stamps were merely printed for philatelic purposes. Genuine used copies probably don't even exist. Tete-beche stamps exist (as in the sheetlet).


1863: 23th issue. Reprints exist of this stamp.


25th issue. The 24 and 25th issue were reprinted. Genuinely used stamps are very rare.
