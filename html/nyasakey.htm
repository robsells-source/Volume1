Stamps in the above design are generally referred to as 'Nyasaland type' and this type was used in many other colonies as well (with changed country name) in order to create some more uniformity in the stamps of the British Colonies. It was mainly used for higher values (and thus mainly for fiscal use) Later on the portrait of King Edward II was replaced by that of King George V, King George VI and even Queen Elizabeth II.


Dealer label of A.Forbin in Paris in the same design, Forbin was a dealier in fiscal stamps and also issued the Forbin catalogue with fiscal stamps.
