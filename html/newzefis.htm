STAMP DUTY


10 Pounds fiscal stamp with embossed background and metal piece attached to it. At the back a black cypher seal with "VR NEW ZEALAND" and crown.

1861 Queen Victoria, inscription "STAMP DUTY"

First issued imperforate and later with perforation:

Many values exist (more than 60), varying from 1 p to 10 Pounds (often in two or more colours, or different value inscriptions); the watermarks used are 'NZ' (small or large), also 'NZ interlaced'. In 1875 the value 1 p violet and green was also issued with watermark 'star'.

In 1874 the type was redone (Plate 2: the dots on the left and right of the circle were done fatter; also white line extends all the way to under "ZE" of "ZEALAND", the vertical background lines were spaced out more).
It seems that some values higher than 10 Pounds (mostly above 1000 Pounds though) were printed on demand in gold color with blue value. Only a few thousand of such stamps were ever made. Example:


An extremely high value: "Twenty-two thousand six hundred and sixty-seven pounds."


Two types of the 1 Sh violet and red; type 1: "ONE SHILLING" in two lines, type 2: "ONE SHILLING" in one line.


1 p with "ONE" printed too far to the left. 1874 type; white line under "ZE" of "ZEALAND", larger white dots on the left and right.

In 1868 some stamps were issued with percentages instead of values ('Ad Valorem', inscription "x P ct" on top, in words at the bottom "x PER CENT"); 1%, 1 1/2 %, 3 %, 5 %, 6 %, 7 % and 10 %; all in brown and all quite rare:


1 % and 3 %.


Forgery, non-existing value: "£14:15:0 FOURTEEN POUNDS FIFTEEN SHILLINGS" blue and red. Probably an 8 p stamp with the red text erased and a new value printed on top of it.

For more infomation about these Stamp Duties, the interested reader is referred to
1) the Forbin catalogue or
2) the Barefoot catalogue of British Commonwealth Revenues.
3) Revenue Catalogue, part 1, Great Britain and colonies by F.G.C. Lundy, 1891.


Some of these stamps were overprinted to be used as postage stamps for Samoa in 1914, for Niue in 1918 and for Rarotonga in 1921.

 

1878 Small sized stamps with image of Queen Victoria


("STAMP DUTY N.Z.", 1 p lilac, issued in 1878)

The 1 p blue exists postally used (this was authorised by the authorities in 1882). It seems that a very limited amount of 1 p lilac stamps was also used postally.

 

1931 Arms type

In this design (with surcharge, 1940), I have also seen the values: "3/6" on 3 Sh 6 p green, "5/6" on 5 Sh 6 p lilac, "11/-" on 11 Sh yellow, "22/-" on 22 Sh red.

Without overprint I have seen the values: 1 Sh 3 p yellow, 1 Sh 3 p yellow and black (value in black), 2 Sh 6 p brown, 4 Sh red, 5 Sh green, 6 Sh red, 7 Sh blue, 8 Sh violet, 9 Sh orange, 10 Sh red, 15 Sh olive, 1 Pound red, 3 Pounds green and 10 Dollars blue.
The values 7 Sh 6 p green, 12 Sh 6 p lilac (1935), 2 Pounds lilac and 5 Pounds blue should also exist.
If I'm well informed these fiscal stamps could also be used for postal purposes.
A stamp with overprint "NOT LIABLE" and no value inscription was issued in 1931.


Overprinted "SAMOA" or "WESTERN SAMOA" for use as postage stamps in Samoa
