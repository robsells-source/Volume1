Forgeries

The Serrane guide mentions some crude forgeries from Nuremberg etc, besides the more advanced forgeries described next.


(Fournier forgeries, except probably the 3 k which might be genuine)

The forger Fournier seems to have made forgeries of these stamps (kreuzer and silbgr), I think only black on coloured paper. Another forger (Oneglia?, Venturini?) printed the forgeries for him in Turin (Italy) on white paper (source Serrane Guide). Fournier then dyed the paper in the required color himself. Fournier offered both mint and used forgeries. In 'The Fournier Album of Philatelic Forgeries' the following numeral cancels can be found "8" (or actually more an inverted "6"?), "95", "195", "142" and "34". I've mostly seen these forgeries in Fournier Albums. They seem to be not common at all. The next image shows a page of a Fournier Album with Thurn and Taxis forgeries:


Album page with Fournier forgeries and forged cancels taken from 'The Fournier Album of Philatelic Forgeries', they were printed with a large black margin all around (which was of course cut off later). The "95" forged cancel (always?) seems to have a part of the outer circle missing.

I suspect that all values were printed in one shot. Since the sheet did not fit in the Fournier Album, the 1/4 and 1/3 Sgr values were cut off and pasted below the sheetlet of the remaining values.

The distinguishing characteristics of the Fournier forgeries are (all of them are quite deceptive, also note non-consistent background colors or too large margins on some stamps):

Since these forgeries were printed individually, they never show any part of adjacent stamps.


1/4 Sgr: background pattern slightly different from the genuine stamps.The bottom right side of the large "4" has a black blob in this forgery. In my view, the line above "Silb." has several consistent weaknesses in this forgery.


1/3 Sgr; part of the bottom left design (just below the right hand side of the horn) appears to be missing and is just a straight line. Also a cancelled stamp with a forged "195" Nastätten cancel.


1/2 Sgr: The bottom of the final "e" of "Freimarke" is different. The green color looks a bit 'off' on some of the forgeries.


1 Sgr: The "S" of "Silb" has a bottom serif in the genuine stamps, it is missing in the Fournier forgeries.


2 Sgr: Some of the lines radiating outwards from the "2" enter the white space to the right of it. The left upper posthorn has some weird 'dent' on the left.


3 Sgr: According to the Serrane Guide the pattern in between the left hand side of the large "3" is not consistent.


1 Kr: The bottom right posthorn is split into two parts in my opinion. Also a cancelled stamp with a forged "195" Nastätten cancel.


3 Kr: In the genuine 3 Kr stamp, there is a curved line above the upper right small "3", this is missing in the Fournier forgery. Also a 'finished forgery' with a forged "143" cancel (Oppenheim).


6 Kr: The ornaments just right to the upper left small "6" are different from the genuine stamps.


9 Kr: Part of the design is missing under the "er" of "Kreuzer". Also a finished forgery with a "95" (Bensheim) cancel. The "95" forged cancel (always?) seems to have a part of the outer circle missing.

As far as I know, Fournier only forged the black stamps on coloured paper.
