Fiscal stamps, overprinted "Judicial" or "JUDICIAL":

The following values exist with this "Judicial" overprint: 3 c brown and black (red overprint), 5 c red and green on yellow (red overprint), 5 c red and black (black overprint), 10 c lilac and black (red overprint; also red and black overprint), 20 c black and lilac (red or black overprint) and 50 c orange and black (red overprint; also red and black overprint).
The values 10 c lilac and black and 50 c orange and black exist with a fiscal "J" overprint (in red on the 10 c) applied in 1901.
The overprint "JUDICIAL" was applied to the value 5 c red and green on yellow, 20 c black and lilac and 50 c orange and black in 1901. Also a 25 c on 50 c orange and black exists (see image above).

Overprint "ED", fiscal use?:

Postal stationary in the same design was also issued: 1 c green, 2 c green, 3 c red, 4 c red and 4 c orange. All of them are postcards except the 4 c orange which is a letter card. Furthermore the surcharges '2 CENTS' on 1 c green and '4 CENTS' on 3 c red exist. A newspaper wrapper 2 c green also exists.

Postal stationery in the same design, but with inscription "REGISTRATION FEE" added:

In this "REGISTRATION" design the values 5 c blue, 10 c blue and 12 c blue exist.

Forged Japanese occupation overprints have appeared from a seller from Florida on Ebay; see http://www.geocities.com/cjyuan00/gallery/fake.htm for more information. They can easily be recognized since the above stamps were never overprinted during the Japanese occupation.

Postage Due stamps


4 c postage due stamp of 1924 with inscriptio "POSTAGE DUE FEDERATED MALAY STATES"

The first postage due stamps for the Federated Malay States were issued in 1924 (inscription "FEDERATED MALAY STATES POSTAGE DUE", 1 c violet, 2 c black, 4 c green, 8 c red, 10 c orange and 12 c blue).


Postage due stamp with inscription "POSTAGE DUE MALAYAN POSTAL UNION"

A second postage due set followed in 1936 with inscription "MALAYAN POSTAL UNION", values: 1 c violet, 1 c brown (1964), 2 c blue (1952), 3 c green (1945), 4 c green, 4 c brown (1965), 5 c red (1945), 8 c red, 8 c orange (1949), 10 c orange, 12 c blue, 12 c lilac (1954), 15 c blue (1945), 20 c blue (1948) and 50 c black. A '10 cents' on 8 c orange was issued in 1965.


With Japanese overprint

During the Japanes occupation, some stamps were overprinted with Japanese characters. I have seen 1 c violet, 3 c green, 4 c green, 5 c red, 9 c orange, 10 c orange, 12 c lilac and 15 c lilac.

I've also seen the values 4 c green, 10 c orange and 12 c lilac with overprint "DAI NIPPON 2602 MALAYA" (also issued during the Japanese occupation).

Railway Stamps

Some railway stamps exist with inscription "FEDERATED MALAY STATES RAILWAYS. NEWSPAPER STAMP", issued in 1918, black text on coloured paper: 1 c, 2 c, 4 c, 5 c, 7 c, 10 c, 10 c, 20 c and 30 c; all with a security number.

Another 5 c stamp with "FMRS" at both sides was issued somewhere in the 1930's. See also https://www.revrevd.com/malaya-fms.html

Fiscal Stamps

Besides the already mentionned postage stamps with overprint "Judicial", "JUDICIAL" or "J", there are also special stamps in the tiger design (as the postage stamps, but larger), but with inscription "JUDICIAL" at the bottom. They were issued in 1903 and the following values exist: 3 c green (center grey), 5 c brown (center grey), 10 c yellow (center brown?), 20 c black on yellow (center grey), 25 c violet (center brown), 50 c light green (center grey), 1 $ orange (center green), 2 $ blue (center green), 3 $ blue (center light green), 5 $ brwon (center green), 10 $ black (center green), 25 $ violet (center green), 50 $ red (center green), 100 $ violet on red (center black).

Postal Stationery

Besides the already mentioned postcards, letter card and newspaper wrapper in the running tiger design (as the postage stamps), another design also exist with a tiger in an embossed oval. The envelopes 4 c orange and 6 c red exist as well as the registered envelope 15 c blue (inscription "REGISTRATION FEE").

MALAYSIA


Example, inscription "PERSEKUTUAN TANAH MELAYU PELAJARAN RENDAH PERCHUMA"

The first stamps of Malaysia (1957) had the inscription "MALAYA" or "PERSEKUTUAN TAHAN MELAYU" (see image above). From 1963 onwards the inscription was "MALAYSIA".

Stamps of Malaysia with inscription "DENDA" are postage due stamps (first issued in 1966).

Stamps with inscription "HASIL PERSUKUTUAN TANAH MELAYU " are fiscal stamps, issued in 1963 (arms in the center in red, blue, gold and yellow), 25 $ (main outer color red), 100 $ (main color blue) and 250 $ (main color green).

Stamps with inscription "HASIL MALAYSIA" were first issued in 1963, again with the arms in multiple colors in the center: 25 $ grey, 100 $ blue, 250 $ red, 500 $ brown and 1000 $ orange. Lower values were also issued later
