In 1964 a set for the Tokyo olympic games was issued with inscription "UGANDA KENYA TANGANYIKA & ZANZIBAR".

Tanganyika and Zanzibar were merged in to the new country Tanzania. The stamps were now inscribed "UGANDA KENYA TANZANIA" with the country names in random order. Each of these countries also issued its own individual stamps.

Kenya Fiscal Stamps


"KODI" stamp in the Nyasaland key type.

In 1934 a fiscal stamp with the portrait of King George V were issued with top-inscription "KODI" and "KENYA" below in the value 1 Sh orange and lilac. A similar stamp with King George VI was also issued (1938, see image above, more values exist: 50 c blue, 2 Sh green and blue, 5 Sh green and black). Stamps with the image of Queen Elizabeth should also exist (I've never seen them): 1 Sh lilac, 2 Sh green, 4 Sh green, 4 Sh orange, 5 Sh gren and black, 5 Sh blue.
Similar Kodi stamps were issued for Tanganyika.


Revenue stamp of Kenya (1963); 10 c green with a standing lion

In this design (standing lion) were issued the values: 10 c green, 20 c lilac, 50 c green, 1 Sh red, 2 Sh blue, 5 Sh orange, 10 Sh green, 20 Sh brown, 25 sh brown (larger size). In 1970 the values 10 c to 20 Sh were re-issued in smaller sizes.

A different standing lion design (but now the lion is facing to the left) was issued in 1982, the inscription reads "KENYA REVENUE".

Again another design (lying lion) with inscription "KENYA REVENUE" was issued in the 1990's.
