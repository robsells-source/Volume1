Many railway stamps exist in Germany. I will present the ones I have seen here, others exist. The book by F.Jünke 'Die deutschen Eisenbahnmarken' has a lot of information concerning these stamps. According to this book, the first railway stamps appeared in 1876 and the last ones towards 1945. The first railway stamps were issued by the Hessische Ludwigs Eisenbahn Gesellschaft. The most common railway stamps are those of Bavaria, Prussia and Wurttemberg. I've seen a lot of uncancelled stamps, mainly of the smaller railways, they might have been issued for collectors purposes only?


1880: Hessische Ludwigs Eisenbahn Gesellschaft 20 p red, 20 p blue and 80 p red. The 20 p red and 80 p red were issued imperforate as well. There is an embossing in the center as well.


1897: "KGL. PREUSS. EISENBAHNDIRECTION FRANKFURT A.M", 20 p blue, a similar design with "MAINZ" also exists.


1900 "KGL. PREUSS. U. GROSSH. HESS STAATSEISENBAHNEN", 20 p blue and olivebrown. A 80 p red and orange and a 30 p green and light green should also exist.


"Grobh Badische Staatseisenb", click also here for more of these stamps. The word "Grobh" was barred in 1919.


"GR. BAD. STAATSEISENBAHN EXPRESSGUT"

With the above inscription "GR. BAD. STAATSEISENBAHN EXPRESSGUT": I've seen the values: 5 p brown, 10 p black, 25 p blue, 30 p green and 50 p red. And with value in "CENTIMES" (for use in the Badischer Bahnhof in Basel, Switzerland): 5 c green, 10 c green, 30 c green, 65 c green, 1 Frank green, 5 Franken green. They should also exist with the inscription "REICHSEISENBAHNEN" and value in "RAPPEN" when the Baden Railways were taken over by the German Reichsbahn, although I haven't seen them..


Inscription "BADISCHE STAATSEISENBAHNEN WERTMARKE". I have seen the values 5p, 10 p and 20 p (all in red with black value inscription).
