Fiscal stamps

Many fiscal stamps were issued for Great Britain. I hereby give a list of the different purposes: admirality, bankruptcy court, board of agriculture, chancery court, civil service, common law courts, companies registration, companines winding up, consular service, contact note, copyhold, customs, district audit, foreign bill, hair powder, in chancery, judicature fees, land commission, land registry, life policy, match tax, matrimonial cuase fee, medicine labels, mixture for coffee, naturalization, patent, paymaster general's service, perfume duty, pedlars' certificate, police courts, probate courts, public records, sea policies, transfer duty.

Some forerunners of fiscal stamps were issued in the 17th century: Duty on Dice, Glove Tax, Hair Powder, Hat Tax and possibly others:


Hat Tax 1784 , Perfume duty 1786, Hair Powder 1786. Images of the Pefrume Duty and Hair Powder obtained from: The Revenue Society Postal Auction 13 May 2023.

Key Types

Some so-called key types were issued for Queen Victoria, King Edward VII, King George V, King George VI and Queen Elizabeth II, where the same basic stamps were used for different fiscal purposes, by overprinting them with the desired fiscal use.

Fiscal stamps for London

"JUSTICE ROOM" (London):


1869 issue, no value indicated.


Justice Room, value written in text


Justice Room, value in red numbers

These Justice Room fiscal stamps were issued from 1869 to 1900.
The first one of 1869 had no value inscription (color red).
In black with red color value I have seen (1888, most values in two types of lettering)
"ONE SHILLING"
"ONE SHILLING AND SIXPENCE"
"TWO SHILLINGS"
"TWO SHILLINGS AND SIXPENCE"
"THREE SHILLINGS", the values 4 Sh, 5 Sh, 10 Sh 6 p and 31 Sh 6 p should also exist
With value in numbers (in red): 1 Sh 0 p black and red (see images above), 2 Sh 0 p blue and red, 2 Sh 0 p black and red, 2 Sh 6 p orange and black, 2 Sh 6 p black and red.
In 1896 the same set was issued with larger numerals and different colors.
Around 1910 the use of these stamps was discontinued.

 

 

"MAYOR's COURT" (London)

The 'Mayor's Court' fiscal stamps were issued from 1883 to 1920, the value is surcharged in red (4 p and 6 p) or black (all other values). Of some values two types exist (second type issued in 1902). The 'cancel' usually consists of a fancy "M C" perfin. The Forbin Guide lists 21 different stamps.
I have seen: "FOUR PENCE" (red value) blue, "SIX PENCE" (black value) blue
and 1/0, 2/0, 3/0, 3/6, 4/0, 4/6, 5/0, 9/6, (all in blue with black value)
and in 1902: 1s0d, 2s0d, 4s0d, 4s6d, 5s0d, 9s6d, (all in blue with black value).

 

"CONSULTATION FEE GUILDHALL", arms of London, used from 1892 to around 1900

2 Sh 6 p ("HALF A CROWN") black; this stamp is rare

Northamptonshire/ Gloucestershire / Isle of Ely: all in design with shield with arms


Inscription 'NORTHAMTONSHIRE', arms (Union Jack in oval) with value. 1870 Blue coloured stamps with red city text and value. 1 p, 6 p, 1 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh, 1 Pound. 1875: "NORTHAMPTON" now in blue, only value in red color (same 7 values). 1882: Green color value in red (same 7 values). A few surcharges (4) exists and a "GAOL" (= prison) overprint (I've never seen them, but they are listed in the Forbin guide, but not in the Walter Morley catalogue). Varieties exist without value inscription


Gloucesterhire; 1 p, 6 p, 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 5 Sh, 10 Sh and 20 Sh. In 1878 issued in blue and red (for justice fees). In 1878 also in lilac and violet (for fines). Finally in 1890 in green and red. Varieties exist without value inscription.
