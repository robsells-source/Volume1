CAMBAY (COOCH BEHAR? KUCH BEHAR?)


Ship design surrounded by Indian text. Issued in Cooch Behar? Listed under Kuch Behar (French spelling) in the Forbin Guide in the values 1 a green, 4 a blue, 8 a red and 1 R violet (issued in 1900). These stamps might actually have been issued in Cambay. If I'm well informed, the value is also indicated at the bottom left (just above the curved part). Thus 1 anna in the first stamp abovce, 4 anna in the second stamp.


1 a green and 4 a grey. Usually printed very badly. If my information is correct, the value was added by hand below the top panel.

Large sized Court Fee stamps also exist for Cambay.

CHANDAWAL


Two Chandawar Court Fee stamps on a document, used in 1944? The red stamp with a different border was issued in Aura.

CHHATARPUR


Normal stamp and overprinted manually in red 4 a on 1 R.


Court Fee 15 a lilac imperforate and 2 R brown perforated. I've also seen 1 a blue, 2 a red, 2 a brown, 3 a violet, 4 a lilac, 4 a brown, 8 a red, 12 a light blue, 15 a lilac, 2 R brown, 5 R orange. Specialists distinghuish two types with the sizes of the lettering of the value inscription different. These stamps also exist with "WAR PURPOSES STAMP" in red or black.


Top parts of stamped papers.

CHHUIKHADAN (CHOOEKHADAN)


Circular design "CHUIKHADAN STATE C.P." and "TALBANA STAMP CHOOEKHADAN ESTATE"

I've seen large sized Court Fee stamps of British India with the image of King George VI with overprint "CHHUIKHADAN STATE" in two lines: 6 a, 12 a, 2 R.

CUTCH (KUTCH)


1880: Cutch; inscription entirely in Gujarati language, 17 values should exists according to the Forbin Guide, ranging from 1/4 Kori to 400 Kori (1/4 Kori = 1 Anna), where this state is listed as Coutch. Forbin says these stamps exists tete-beche. I presume the value is indicated in the center line. I think the first stamp is the 1/4 Kori value


Circular design, black.

DEWAS (DEVAS)


Dewas Junior (the "2" stands for Junior). This stamp also exists with inscription "DEVAS" instead of "DEWAS". This stamp was printed in black, red, yellow and green. It is probably the only fiscal stamp of India, printed in so many colors.


Similar type, but in red color.
