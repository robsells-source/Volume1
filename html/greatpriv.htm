Liverpool Cotton Brokers' Association

The Walter Morley catalogue of 1897 lists these stamps on page 161. If my information is correct, these stamps were issued from 1875 to 1910.


1875: 6 p red and 1878: 1 Sh black; two types; without or with dot behind "SHILLING" and ornaments in the corners different size? .


1882: With perfin "C H".


Blue (1883), black on blue (1883), black on yellow (1888); I've also seen "5/-" on 1 Sh blue ("CLEARING HOUSE) and "5/-" on 2 Sh red ("ARBITRATION ROOM")..


1883: "LIVERPOOL COTTON ASSOCIATION LIMITED CLEARING HOUSE"


1883(?): "ARBITRATION", 6 p green. A 6 p red with "DECLARATION" should also exist.


1 Pound brown ("COTTON BROKERS" inscription)., 1.10 Pound brown (but with "COTTON" inscription, 2 Pound, 2 Sh yellow used in 1883 and a red stamp in the same value used in 1889.

Liverpool Corn Trade Association

The Walter Morley catalogue of 1897 lists these stamps on page 161.


1897: Value on top


1897 with value at the bottom. Two types exist, with "LIMITED" or "LIMD" in the text. These stamps were in use upto 1975.

B.S.A. Clearing Department

Walter Morley only lists one stamp, a 1 sh blue.


1896: B.S.A.: Beetroot Sugar Association (Liverpool). In 1899 a very similar stamp was issued, but with a different background pattern.

Tea Clearing House

1924 Inscription "TEA CLEARING HOUSE CHARGES PAID"

I have seen:
Without overprint: 1/4 p violet, 1/2 p grey, 1 p red, 1 1/2 p lilac, 2 p blue, 3 p green, 3 1/2 p light brown, 4 p violet, 6 p orange, 7 p green, 7 p orange, 1 Sh yellow, 1 Sh 2 p brown.
With lozenges overprint: 1/2 p grey, 1 p red, 2 p blue, 3 p green, 4 p violet, 6 p orange, 7 p green, 7 p light brown, 1 Sh yellow, 1 Sh 2 p brown
With triangle overprint: 1/4 p violet, 1/2 p grey, 1 p red, 1 1/2 p lilac, 2 p blue, 3 p green, 3 1/2 p light brown, 6 p orange
With red surcharge "95 PER CENT": 1 p red, 2 p blue, 3 p green 4 p violet, 6 p orange.
