Fiscal stamps

Many fiscal stamps were issued for Great Britain. I hereby give a list of the different purposes: admirality, bankruptcy court, board of agriculture, chancery court, civil service, common law courts, companies registration, companies winding up, consular service, contact note, copyhold, customs, district audit, foreign bill, hair powder, in chancery, judicature fees, land commission, land registry, life policy, match tax, matrimonial cuase fee, medicine labels, mixture for coffee, naturalization, patent, paymaster general's service, perfume duty, pedlars' certificate, police courts, probate courts, public records, sea policies, transfer duty.

Great Britain Fiscal stamps, Consular Service to Excise Revenue

 

"FOREIGN BILL"

The first 'foreign bill' stamps appeared in 1854; 1 p, 2 p, 3 p, 4 p, 6 p, 8 p, 1 Sh, 1 Sh 4 p, 1 Sh 8 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 3 Sh, 3 Sh 4 p, 4 Sh, 5 Sh, 6 Sh 8 p, 7 Sh 6 p, 10 Sh, 13 Sh 4 p, 15 Sh, 1 Pound, 1.10 Pounds, 2 Pounds and 2 1/2 Pounds, all in the colour lilac.

In 1856 a similar set was issued, but now with red letters (the main colour still lilac) in the values: 1 p, 2 p, 3 p, 4 p, 6 p, 8 p, 9 p, 1 Sh, 1 Sh 4 p, 1 Sh 8 p, 2 Sh, 2 Sh 6 p, 3 Sh, 4 Sh 4 p, 4 Sh, 5 Sh, 6 Sh 8 p, 7 Sh 6 p, 10 Sh, 13 Sh 4 p, 15 Sh, 1 Pound, 1.10 Pounds, 2 Pounds, 2.10 Pounds, 2.5 Pounds and 5 Pounds.


1854 and 1856 issue. Similar stamps with value in "ANNAS" or "RUPEES" were issued for India.

In 1860 in the red crown design three stamps were issued (surcharged "FOREIGN BILL" in brown): 2.10 Pounds, 5 Pounds and 10 Pounds.

In 1871 a new design was issued (with a color change in 1872, more values in a similar design followed in 1881):


1871 issue; 1 p, 2 p, 3 p, 6 p, 9 p (all in lilac); 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 3 Sh, 4 Sh, 5 Sh, 10 Sh (all in lilac); 1 Pound, 1 Pound 10 Sh, 2 Pounds, 2 Pounds 10 Sh, 5 Pounds (all in green)


1872 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 3 Sh, 10 Sh, 15 Sh (all in green, also bluish?); 1 Pound, 1 Pound 10 Sh, 2 Pounds, 2 Pounds 10 Sh, 5 Pounds (all in lilac)


1881; designs all different; 1 Sh, 2 Sh, 3 Sh, 4 Sh, 5 Sh, 10 Sh, 15 Sh (all in green);

"HAT TAX (HAT DUTY)"

1784 For tax on men's hats. This label had to be pasted inside the hat.


6 p red, lion on the left, unicorn on the right, crown in the center "STAMP OFFICE VALUE SEVEN SHILLINGS AND ABOVE FOUR SHILLINGS" Size 73 x 34 mm

3 p red (2 types): for hats less expensive than 4 Shillings
6 p red or black; for hats costing less than 7 Shilings and more than 4 Shilings
1 Sh red: for hats from 7 to 12 Shillings
2 Sh red (2 types): for hats costing more than 12 shillings
3 Sh red: usage:?

See: http://branchesofmytree.weebly.com/genealogy-blog/56-strange-taxes-2-the-hat-tax for more details.

"LAND COMMISSION"


5 Pounds lilac and green, here with "SPECIMEN" overprint.

1886: 6 values, key types, inscription "THE LAND COMMISSION": 1 Sh green and black, 2 Sh 6 p green and brown, 5 Sh green and lilac, 10 Sh green and red.
Larger size: 1 Pound lilac and black, 5 Pounds lilac and green.
These stamps are very rare (more common with "SPECIMEN" overprint). Similar stamps exist for Ireland with inscription "LAND COMMISSION IRELAND".

In 1889, these stamps were replace by Board of Agriculture stamps.
