WECHSEL STEMPEL (Bill of exchange)

1871 Inscription "DEUTSCHER WECHSEL STEMPEL", value in "GROSCHEN"

The following values were issued (stamps blue, value in grey or black): 1 g, 1 1/2 g, 3 g, 4 1/2 g, 6 g, 7 1/2 g, 9 g, 12 g, 15 g, 22 1/2 g, 30 g, 45 g, 60 g, 90 g, 150 g and 300 g.
The 1 1/2 g and 3 g seem to exist with erroneous inscription "NORDDEUTSCHER" instead of "DEUTSCHER".

I've seen a sheet with 45 300 g stamps on it, part of a French war debt of 300,000 Thalers after the French German war, which was paid in Paris 1st March 1872. The text on the backside reads:
"Monsieur P. Bleichroder a Berlin
Nous vous prions de vouloir bien, a partir du quinze Mars courant,
tenir a la disposition du Tresor Francais vu de la personne
qui vous sera designee par qui, contre remise de la presente
delegation dument acuittee la somme de
trois millions cinq cent mille thalers de Prusse, dont il vous
plaira debiter notre compte, suivant notre avis
Agreez monsieur, nos sinceres salutations."
(Signed by the minister of Finance)

Similar stamps, but with inscription "NORDDEUTSCHER WECHSEL STEMPEL" were issued for the North German Federation. ':

1901 color change and 1901 new design for higher values

In 1900 the "ten .... 18" was removed from the bottom and only "den" remained,
I have seen M0,10, M0,20 (both in green), M.0,30, M.0,40, M.0,50, M.1,00, M.2,00, M.3,00, M.5,00, M.10,00 and M.15,00 also exist.
In 1901 the colors were changed: M.1,00, M.1,50, M.2,00, M.2,50, M.3,00, M.3,50, M.4,00, M.4,50, M.5,00 (all in violet).
Furthermore (1901) M.10,00, M.15,00 (see image above), M.18,00, M.20,00, M.25,00, M.30,00 and M.50,00 (all in red with green underprint), the values M.100,00 and M.500,00 should also exist.


1916 issue with fancy "M"

In 1916, the "M." was changed to a fancy "M" without a dot:
In green with black text: M0,15, M0,30, M0,45, M0,60
In blue with black text: M1,20, M1,80, M2,40, M3,00, M3,60, M4,20, M4,80, M5,40, M6,00
In violet and green with black text: M12,00, M18,00, M24,00, M30,00, M36,00, M60,00, M120,00, M600,00.

In 1919 some values were overprinted with a diagonal "SAARGEBIET".
I've also seen stamps for Danzig with "Danzig" overprint.
