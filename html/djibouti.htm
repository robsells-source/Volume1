The 25 F and 50 F are known to have been forged (by Fournier?). The "F" has a triangular central part instead of a square part. In the 50 F, the "0" is of the same size as the "5" in the forgeries (it should be considerably smaller).


Fournier forgery

A forged surcharged "0,40" on 4 c is described in Le Postillon Revue Philatelique of 1912, page 222. The 25 F and 50 F forgeries are described in the same journal 1902, page 8.

Inverted centers:


(Inverted centers, examples)

Many stamps with inverted centers exist. An official of the Agency of the Colonies in Paris was responsable for checking stamp sheets for errors. A stamp dealer asked him if he could buy some of the 'errors' the official had discovered. This dealer then sold these rarities to stamp collectors. This fascinating story can also be read in 'The truth about the stamps of the Somali Coast with inverted centers' (source: http://www.apfelbauminc.com/library/SomaliCoast.htm, translated from 'L’Echo de la Timbrologie' and published in the 'American Journal of Philately' in 1905). Later on, this official even printed complete sheets with inverted centers, to sell them to stamp dealers. The market was then flooded with thousands of these inverted center stamps!
The forger Fournier also offered these 'errors'. In 'The Fournier album of philatelic forgeries' a block of 4 c stamps with two of them with inverted centers can be found. Also a piece of a letter with two stamps; a 2 F inverted and a 2 F normal. All these 'errors' are cancelled with "DJIBOUTI COTE FRANCAISE DES SOMALIS 25 JANV" (with a blotch of ink where normally the year would be).
