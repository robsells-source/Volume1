Fiscal stamps


I've been told this Indian stamp with a diamond shaped overprint with "S.S." in the center is a fiscal stamp for Straits Settlements issued in 1863, I could not find any information confirming this.

REVENUE:

1874 Large sized stamps, "REVENUE" on top, "STAMP" below

The following values were issued: 3 c, 5 c, 10 c, 15 c, 20 c, 25 c, 30 c, 50 c, 60 c (all in lilac, value in black), $1, $2, $3, $5, $10, $25 and $50 (all in blue, value in black).

1882 New smaller design


reduced sized image, with typical red cancel often found on fiscal stamps of Straits Settlements, the red cancel indicates the same value as the stamp together with the date 25 9 88 in this case.

The following values exist:
A: With value in colour of the stamp (1882): 3 c, 5 c, 10 c, 15 c, 20 c, 25 c, 30 c, 50 c, 60 c (all in lilac), $1, $2, $3, $5, $10, $25 and $50 (all in blue)
B: With value in other colour: 3 c lilac and black , 5 c lilac and blue, 10 c lilac (two shades on one stamp), 20 c lilac and green, 50 c lilac and red, $1 green and red, $2 green and blue, $5 green and violet, $25 green and $100 green and red.

(Receipt stamp of 1875)

Straits Settlements issued its own receipt stamps in two values: 3 c lilac (1875) or 3 c violet (1876).
