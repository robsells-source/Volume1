Erasmo Oneglia was an Italian forger in the late 19th century from Turin. He is famous for his engraved forgeries, but he also made photo-lithographed forgeries. Many of his forgeries were previously attributed to the forger Angelo Panelli. The engraved Oneglia forgeries are usually 'overinked'. When Oneglia applied a watermark on his forgeries, it is very clearly visible. His watermarks are impressed instead of being real watermarks. Oneglia also collaborated closely with A.Venturini and E.Spiotti. The exact relationships that these forgers had is not clear up to this day.

The 10 c 1864 forgeries are described in 'De vervalsingen van Nederland & O.G.' by F.P.A. van de Loo (in Dutch). It is there refererred to as a 'Oniglia' forgery. The margins of these forgeries are often very large. They can be found with cancels that were also used on other Oneglia forgeries (bars with "G" cancel or 5 concentric rings).
A forgery of the 5 c postage due stamp is also described in the van de Loo book, with smudges in the "P" and "R" of "PORT".. It is cancelled with a numeral "3" cancel. I do not know if this is the same forgery as the one from Oneglia, (there are two forgery types listed in the van de Loo book). Further confirmation of this would be that I've seen a large Russian Levant stamp with a numeral "3" cancel (see there).
