CITY MAIL FREE STAMP

City Mail Free Stamp


Image obtained from http://www.siegelauctions.com/2000/830/y830119.htm

Issued for New York in 1845, extremely rare, only 6 stamps are known to have survived. The only value issued was black on grey (no value indication).

CLARK & CO

Clark & Co. Mailbox, one cent


(Genuine, image obtained from a Siegel auction)

This stamp was issued in 1857 in red on yellow in New York (no other colours!). Note that similar stamps exist with the inscription "Brady & Co" (the company was sold to Abner S. Brady). .

Forgeries and reprints:

Some forgeries were produced by S. Allan Taylor, Hussey and others, also in many colours not corresponding to the original red on yellow. Examples:


Some forgeries with the "O" of "ONE" as in the genuine stamp (most forgeries have the "O" different). Note that there is a break in the circle above the "AR" of "CLARKE". Squarish dot behind "CENT".


Another forgery with the "O" of "ONE" as in the genuine stamps.


a minisheet of 'reprints'. I have seen a larger sheet with 4 rows of 5 stamps, 2 rows printed upside down and with a quite large space in between.

Another forgery.

CLARK & HALL

Clark & Hall


(Reduced size, image obtained from a Siegel auction)

This stamp (1 c black on lilac) was issued in 1851. These stamps are extremely rare, only 5 stamps have survived.

CLARKE'S EXPRESS

Clarke's Express (New York)


The design of this stamp seems to consist of horizontal lines.


(Genuine, reduced sizes, images obtained from a Siegel auction)

Around 1865 this stamp with the design of George Washington was issued in two colours: blue and black. Both are extremely rare.


Forgery of the black stamp. The text at the left hand side is inverted.

CLINTON'S PENNY POST

Clinton's Penny Post Philadelphia


Genuine

Only a 1 c black was issued. This stamp is extremely rare (only two? copies have survived). I presume the stamps below are forgeries. Due to the amount of forgeries, this issue was longtime presumed to be a bogus issue. I do not know how many forgeries exist (at least five different types).


Rather deceptive forgeries. Note the similar break in the upper frame (above "TO") and the dot on top of the right hand side of the "O" of "POST" in the first two stamps. Also note the similar smudges on the last two stamps. Several sub-types appear to exist.


No apostrophe after "N" of "CLINTON", but from the same maker as the stamps above?


First "P" of "PHILADELPHIA" has a too small upper part.

I've also seen bogus colour in red or violet, etc.

COOK'S DISPATCH

Cook's Dispatch


(Genuine, image obtained from a Siegel auction)

Only 5 stamps of this green stamp are known to exist (3 on cover). They were issued in 1853 in Baltimore.

CORNWELL

1856 Cornwell Post Office Madison Square


(Genuine, image obtained from a Siegel auction)

Two stamps were issued, a red one and a red on blue one (both no value indicated). They were issued in 1856 in New York.

The above forgery was printed by Thomas Woods in 1866 for George Hussey. A total of 3000 of these forgeries were printed.


Another primitive forgery, note the small mouth. I've seen this forgery in the colors red, green and lilac. The "M" of MADISON" is under the "NW" of "CORNWELL". These forgeries were made by Taylor and Deraedemaeker (the engraver of Moens), the only difference being that the circles in the corners were better done by the Moens forgery.


Not sure what is this, very blurly printed and bottom circles missing.

CRESSMAN & CO

Cressman & Co's Penny Post Philad'a


(Genuine, image obtained from a Siegel auction)

In 1856 Cressman & Co's Penny Post issued two stamps: a gold on black stamp and a gold on lilac stamp.

The next stamps are forgeries, the text "CRESSMAN & CO'S" is too wide (also note the position of the "D" of "PHILAD" with respect to the "P" of "POST", or the "C" of "CRESSMAN" with respect to the "P" of "PENNY".


Hussey? reprints. Printed in sheetlets of 12 with 6 sub-types. In the first sub-type the "&" is almost entirely missing.

CROSBY'S CITY POST

Crosby's City Post


I've been told that the first stamp is genuine. The second one is with cancel.


The block at the right shows the printer's name: J.W.Scott. Note the damaged right bottom corner of the left bottom stamp. A full sheet consists of 25 (5x5) stamps.

Issued in 1870, the only value that exist is a 2 c red for O.H.Crosby, 19 William Street. This stamp is not very rare in unused condition, it was printed by J.W.Scott & Co (!). Nevertheless forgeries exist:

Forgeries, examples:


Taylor forgeries in violet and green, I've also seen this forgery in red, orange, brown and blue.

For other Taylor forgeries in other colours (brown and blue) click here. The Taylor forgeries resemble quite closely the genuine design, except that the letters are slightly different (see for example the "O" of "CROSBY"). Also, in my opinion, the "1" of "19" has it top serif pointing upwards, while it is pointing downwards in the genuine stamps I've seen. .


Other forgery, similar in design to the Browne&Co local stamps. I've also seen this forgery in the color blue and brown.

I have also seen the above forgery in the colour black.


A bogus issue for Crossby's Special Message Post 29 William St. I've also seen it in black on yellow color. The design was based on the (also bogus) Galveston confederate stamp. Presumably these forgeries were made by Taylor. At some stage, these stamps were believed to be genuine.

CUMMINGS CITY POST

Cummings City Post


(Genuine stamps, reduced sizes, images obtained from a Siegel auction)

A.H. Cummings' City Post operated in New York and started issuing stamps in 1844. Three values in the above design were issued: 2 c black on lilac, 2 c black on green and 2 c black on yellow.
