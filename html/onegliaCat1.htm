Erasmo Oneglia was an Italian forger in the late 19th century from Turin. He is famous for his engraved forgeries, but he also made photo-lithographed forgeries. Many of his forgeries were previously attributed to the forger Angelo Panelli. The engraved Oneglia forgeries are usually 'overinked'. When Oneglia applied a watermark on his forgeries, it is very clearly visible. His watermarks are impressed instead of being real watermarks. Oneglia also collaborated closely with A.Venturini and E.Spiotti. The exact relationships that these forgers had is not clear up to this day.


Title page of the Oneglia catalogue from 1897-98. Next to it the 1897 Spiotti catalogue, which looks remarkably similar.

Oneglia writes with regards to who made the forgeries he is offering in his 1906 pricelist:
"Les imitations distingués avec un * sont plus parfaites des autres et plus recommandées ; - et celles signés avec un N ne sont pas de ma édition, par conséquence elles sont pas bien faites comme les autres."
Translation: the forgeries marked with "*" are more perfect than the other and are more recommended. Those marked "N" are not made by me, and consequently are not as well done as the others.

There are a number of forgery types known of these stamps of Ethiopia. The well documented ones are the Fournier forgeries and the Kamigata forgeries (with "IMITATION") cancel. The other forgeries usually have a square with lines as cancel. I presume it is these last ones that are the Oneglia forgeries.
